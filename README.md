# C25 · Crunch Founders — Zero to Launch

> A free, open-source **founder/startup track**: 10 weeks from "I have an idea, I think?" to "I validated a real problem with real people, built something they can use, and pitched it in three minutes without flinching." Built around customer discovery, the lean build-measure-learn loop, honest co-founder agreements, and the early-stage decisions that quietly decide whether a company survives.

C25 is a **course**, not an accelerator and not a get-rich pitch. It teaches the boring, decisive skills that early-stage founders almost always learn too late: talking to customers before building, splitting equity before resentment sets in, knowing your unit economics before you scale a money-loser, and reading a term sheet before you sign one. The course lives in `curriculum/`.

---

## Who this is for

- You are a student, engineer, or operator who keeps having startup ideas and wants a disciplined way to test one without quitting your job first.
- You have a co-founder (or want one) and you have not yet had the hard conversations about roles, equity, and control.
- You can build, but you have never validated — you ship features no one asked for and call it product work.
- You are a club organizer who wants a curriculum to run a 10-week founder cohort.

If you want to learn to *build* the product itself, this isn't that course — see [C8](../C8-CRUNCH-LABS-WEB-DEV/) or [C16](../C16-CRUNCH-PRO-WEB-BACKEND/). C25 is *about* the founder's job: deciding what is worth building and for whom, and surviving the people decisions around it.

---

## What you will be able to do at the end of 10 weeks

- **Tell founder-market fit from wishful thinking** — and say honestly whether *you* are the right person for the problem you picked.
- **Generate and triage startup ideas** against a problem-first filter instead of a solution-first one.
- **Run a real customer-discovery interview** without leading the witness, and turn ten of them into a pattern you can act on.
- **Have the co-founder conversation** — roles, decision rights, equity split, and vesting with a cliff — before money or ego makes it impossible.
- **Scope and ship a lean MVP** that tests one risky assumption, then run a build-measure-learn loop on the result.
- **Sketch a business model and compute unit economics** (CAC, LTV, contribution margin, payback) well enough to know if the model can ever work.
- **Choose a go-to-market motion** and define the one early-traction metric that matters for your stage.
- **Read the fundraising landscape** — bootstrapping vs. angels vs. pre-seed — and model how a cap table dilutes across a round.
- **Understand incorporation, founder vesting, and a basic co-founder agreement** well enough to brief a lawyer instead of being briefed by one.
- **Deliver a 3-minute pitch** that survives questions, backed by evidence rather than adjectives.

The capstone is **a validated startup concept** — real discovery evidence, a lean model, a working MVP or clickable prototype, a deck, and a recorded pitch — committed to your portfolio.

## Prerequisites

- **No business degree required.** No prior startup experience required.
- **Willingness to talk to strangers.** The course is interview-driven; the capstone needs at least ten real customer-discovery conversations. This is non-negotiable.
- **Comfortable with Markdown and Git/GitHub** — you will commit deliverables each week. If you are new to Git, skim [C4](../C4-Crunch-Labs-Hackathons/)'s prerequisites first.
- **Some way to build a rough MVP** — code, no-code, or a clickable prototype. You do not need to be a strong engineer; you need to be resourceful.

## Two formats

| Format | Length | Cadence | Best for |
|--------|--------|---------|----------|
| **Cohort** | 10 weeks · ~10 hrs/wk | The default — a group moving together, sharing interviews and demo days | A club or class running a founder track end to end |
| **Solo sprint** | Self-paced · ~100 hrs total | One founder working through the same deliverables alone | A builder testing one idea on their own schedule |

## Toolkit (all free or free-tier)

| Tool | Role |
|------|------|
| **A notebook or doc tool** (Markdown, Google Docs, Notion free) | Interview notes, model, deck outline |
| **Spreadsheet** (Google Sheets, LibreOffice Calc) | Cap table, unit economics, CAC/LTV |
| **A landing-page builder** (Carrd free, plain HTML, GitHub Pages) | Smoke-test / fake-door MVP |
| **No-code / low-code** (Google Forms, Airtable free, Glide free) | Concierge and Wizard-of-Oz MVPs |
| **Figma free** | Clickable prototype |
| **OBS Studio / Loom free** | Recording the 3-minute pitch |
| **Slides** (Google Slides, reveal.js, Marp) | The pitch deck |
| **GitHub** | Versioning every deliverable; the portfolio home |

We deliberately avoid any single paid stack. The *patterns* — discovery, the lean loop, unit economics, vesting — transfer no matter which tool you reach for.

## What you ship

By the end of the 10 weeks you publish, in a public repo:

1. **A customer-discovery dossier** — ten or more interview write-ups and the synthesized pattern across them.
2. **A lean business model canvas** — nine blocks, each defensible against your evidence.
3. **An MVP or clickable prototype** — the smallest thing that tests your riskiest assumption, with the result.
4. **A unit-economics sheet** — CAC, LTV, contribution margin, and payback, with stated assumptions.
5. **A co-founder agreement draft** — roles, decision rights, equity split, and a vesting schedule with a cliff (template, not legal advice).
6. **A pitch deck and a 3-minute recorded pitch** — the demo-day artifact you can re-use anywhere.
