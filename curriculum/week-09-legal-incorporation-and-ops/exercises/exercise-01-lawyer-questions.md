# Exercise 1 — Lawyer & Accountant Questions

**Goal:** Turn this week's concepts into a specific, prioritized list of questions for a first meeting with a lawyer and an accountant, so the meeting is cheap and useful.

**Estimated time:** 45 minutes.

---

## What to produce

Create `exercises/exercise-01-lawyer-questions.md` with two short lists, each tailored to *your* venture (not generic):

```markdown
# Advisor questions — <your venture>

## For a lawyer
1. Given my location and fundraising plans, what entity should I form?
2. How should founder stock and vesting be papered?
3. Do all founders/contributors have IP assignment in place? What about work done before we agreed?
4. ...

## For an accountant
1. Are there tax elections tied to founder stock with deadlines I must not miss?
2. What ongoing filings/taxes will the entity owe?
3. ...

## Known traps in my situation
(Anything from the trap-spotting activity that applies to you — e.g., code built on a personal account.)
```

---

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] File present; at least four lawyer questions and three accountant questions.
- [ ] Questions are tailored to your venture, not generic.
- [ ] A "known traps in my situation" section.
- [ ] Committed.

---

## Hints

- The most valuable questions come from honestly examining how your work and team came together so far. Was anything built before agreements existed?
- "What should I incorporate as?" is fine; "Given that I plan to raise a pre-seed and live in X, what entity fits?" gets a better answer.
