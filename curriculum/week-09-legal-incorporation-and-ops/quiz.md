# Week 9 — Quiz

Five questions. Notes closed. Answer key at the bottom.

---

**Q1.** A primary reason to incorporate is:

- A) It makes the logo look official.
- B) Liability separation, ownership clarity, and fundraising readiness.
- C) It is required to talk to customers.
- D) It eliminates all taxes.

**Q2.** Why does the course refuse to prescribe a single entity type?

- A) Entities don't matter.
- B) The right choice is jurisdiction- and situation-specific and belongs with a professional.
- C) All entities are identical.
- D) It is illegal to discuss.

**Q3.** IP assignment ensures that:

- A) Investors own the product.
- B) The work (code, designs, brand) legally belongs to the company, not to whoever made it.
- C) The founders cannot be fired.
- D) Customers cannot copy the product.

**Q4.** Mixing personal and company money in one account:

- A) Is fine for small companies.
- B) Creates tax and liability headaches (commingling) and should be avoided.
- C) Is required by most banks.
- D) Reduces dilution.

**Q5.** The healthy boundary between you and a professional is:

- A) Do everything yourself to save money.
- B) Outsource everything, including your founders' agreement draft.
- C) Organize and prepare yourself; get binding documents and entity/tax choices from a professional.
- D) Wait until a problem arises, then call a lawyer.

---

## Answer key

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

1. **B** — liability, ownership, fundraising readiness. (L1 §1)
2. **B** — jurisdiction-specific; advisor territory. (L1 §3)
3. **B** — the company owns the work. (L1 §5)
4. **B** — commingling is a trap; keep accounts separate. (L2 §2)
5. **C** — prepare yourself, get binding docs from a pro. (L2 §3)

</details>
