# Mini-Project — The Incorporation & Ops Checklist

> Produce a tailored incorporation and operations checklist for *your* concept — the one you've carried since Week 1 — with every jurisdiction- or situation-specific item flagged for a real advisor. It is the artifact that proves you know what a clean, fundable company setup requires and exactly where you need professional help. This is the Week 9 mini-deliverable from the syllabus, brought to full depth.

**This is education, not legal or tax advice. Anything binding or jurisdiction-specific is flagged for a licensed professional — that flagging is part of the deliverable, not a cop-out.**

**Estimated time:** ~7.5 hours, spread across the week (the bulk on Friday/Saturday, with inputs assembled from the exercises and challenge earlier in the week).

---

## What you're building and why it compounds

By Week 9 you have a stack of artifacts: a founder brief (W1), discovery interviews (W3), a co-founder agreement draft with vesting (W4), an MVP (W5), a lean canvas and unit economics (W6), a GTM one-pager (W7), and a cap-table model with post-round dilution (W8). This mini-project is the one that turns all of that paper into a **company that could actually take money** — and it's the direct input to next week's pitch, where "is this a clean company?" is one of the obvious hard questions.

Concretely, this checklist pulls together:

- The **Week 4 vesting** → how it becomes issued, restricted founder stock.
- The **Week 8 cap table** → the ownership the entity will actually register.
- This week's **Exercise 1** (advisor questions), **Exercise 2** (IP-trap audit), **Exercise 3** (incorporation canvas), and the **challenge** (due-diligence triage).

You are not starting from a blank page. You're assembling the week's outputs into one coherent, defensible plan.

---

## What you will produce

A single file, `ops/INCORPORATION-OPS-CHECKLIST.md` in your venture repo, with **six sections**:

### 1. Incorporation steps (sequenced, each flagged DIY or advisor)

The ordered sequence for *your* situation:

```
Form entity → issue founder stock with vesting → everyone signs IP assignment
→ file the time-sensitive tax election (on deadline) → open dedicated bank account
→ start bookkeeping → store signed docs + cap table in one place → track deadlines
```

Each step marked **[DIY]** (you do it) or **[ADVISOR]** (lawyer/accountant), with a one-line "why" for the advisor ones. Sequenced for your case, not generic — if you're cross-border, that changes the order and the flags.

### 2. Agreements register

A table of every agreement you need, who signs each, and its status (**have / need / later**):

| Agreement | Who signs | When | Status |
|-----------|-----------|------|--------|
| Founders' agreement (stock + vesting) | … | … | … |
| IP / invention assignment | … | … | … |
| Advisor agreement(s) | … | … | … |
| Contractor / employee agreements (IP + confidentiality) | … | … | … |
| NDA (only if truly needed) | … | … | … |

### 3. IP & trap fixes

The output of your Exercise 2 audit and the challenge: each classic trap, its present state in your company, and the fix — with the cheap "fix now" items separated from the `[NEEDS ADVISOR]` ones.

### 4. Ops minimum

Deliberately minimal: the dedicated bank account, the bookkeeping approach, the one document store, and the compliance/deadline note (including the time-sensitive tax election). If it's not solving a problem you have *today*, it doesn't belong here.

### 5. Advisor questions

The lawyer and accountant question lists from Exercise 1, refined — the questions that make your first professional meeting cheap and useful.

### 6. Not-advice note

A visible statement that this is a planning checklist, not legal or tax advice, and that binding/jurisdiction-specific items go to professionals.

## Suggested order of operations

You'll find this far easier if you assemble it from the week's outputs rather than writing it cold.

### Phase 1 — Pull your inputs together (~0.5h)
Open, side by side: your Week 4 co-founder agreement, your Week 8 cap table, and this week's Exercise 1, 2, and 3 and the challenge triage. Most of the checklist already exists scattered across these; this project organizes it.

### Phase 2 — Sequence the incorporation steps (~1.5h)
Write the ordered steps for *your* situation. Flag each `[DIY]` or `[ADVISOR]`. The cross-border, entity-choice, and tax-election items are almost always `[ADVISOR]`; the bank account, bookkeeping, and document-store items are `[DIY]`. Pull the entity reasoning from Exercise 3.

### Phase 3 — Build the agreements register (~1h)
List every agreement (Lecture 2, §1), who signs, when, and current status. Be honest about "need" vs. "have" — most early companies have zero signed and need most.

### Phase 4 — Carry over the IP/trap fixes (~1h)
Paste in the triaged output of Exercise 2 and the challenge. Separate the one-page-signature fixes from the `[NEEDS ADVISOR]` ones. Assign owners and dates to the cheap fixes.

### Phase 5 — Write the ops minimum (~1h)
The shortest list that keeps the company clean: dedicated bank account, bookkeeping approach, one document home, deadline calendar (with the tax election on it). Resist adding tools you don't need yet.

### Phase 6 — Refine the advisor questions + not-advice note (~1h)
Bring in and tighten the Exercise 1 lists. Add the visible not-advice statement.

### Phase 7 — Consistency pass + commit (~1.5h)
Read the whole thing as if you were the investor's lawyer. Does the founder stock match the Week 4 vesting? Does the cap table match Week 8? Is every binding item flagged? Fix any inconsistency, then commit.

## Worked example (abridged)

For the clinic-scheduling founder from Lecture 1, the checklist's incorporation-steps section might read:

```
1. [ADVISOR] Confirm entity choice — cross-border (co-founder in another country),
   planning a pre-seed SAFE. Lawyer call booked for <date>.
2. [ADVISOR] Form the entity once choice confirmed.
3. [DIY → ADVISOR papers it] Issue founder stock 55/45, restricted, 4-yr / 1-yr cliff
   (matches Week 4 + Week 8 cap table).
4. [ADVISOR] File the time-sensitive tax election with accountant — HARD DEADLINE,
   on compliance calendar the day stock is granted.
5. [DIY] Everyone signs IP assignment (incl. the weekend-script friend — chasing his
   signature this week; he's reachable).
6. [DIY] Open dedicated business bank account; stop using personal card for tools.
7. [DIY] Start bookkeeping spreadsheet.
8. [DIY] One "Company Docs" drive folder for agreements + cap table.
9. [DIY] Compliance calendar with tax election + annual filing deadlines.
```

Yours will differ — that's the point. A solo bootstrapper's checklist looks very different from a two-country venture-track team's.

## Rubric

| Criterion | Weight | What "great" looks like |
|-----------|-------:|-------------------------|
| Steps sequenced and correctly flagged DIY vs. advisor | 30% | The order fits *this* venture; binding/jurisdiction items are all `[ADVISOR]`, organizing items are `[DIY]`; no binding item answered with a guess |
| Agreements register complete with signers and status | 25% | Every needed agreement listed with who signs and have/need/later; nothing essential missing |
