# Week 9 — Resources

Every resource on this page is **free** and reputable. Several are the *actual* legal documents real startups use (YC publishes theirs openly). None of this is legal or tax advice; it is reading that makes you a better-prepared client for a real professional in your jurisdiction.

A note on geography: most freely available startup-legal writing is **US-centric** (Delaware C-corp, 83(b), SAFEs). If you are not founding in the US, treat the US material as a *model of how to think*, not a template to copy. The questions transfer; the specific entity, election, and filing do not. Find your local equivalent and your local professional.

## Founder stock, vesting & tax elections

- **Carta — learn library ("founder stock," "vesting," "cliff")** — free, lawyer-reviewed primers that tie back to your Week 4 vesting schedule:
  <https://carta.com/learn/>
- **Cooley GO — equity & founder-stock explainers** — a major startup law firm's free knowledge base:
  <https://www.cooleygo.com/documents/>
- **US IRS — small business & self-employed (Section 83(b) context)** — the canonical time-sensitive election on restricted founder stock; read to understand the *deadline and irreversibility*, then file it *with an accountant*, never alone:
  <https://www.irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed>
- **Investopedia — "Vesting"** — quick conceptual refresher:
  <https://www.investopedia.com/terms/v/vesting.asp>

> **Read this twice:** the 83(b)-style election is the single most common avoidable, irreversible, expensive founder mistake. Its analogue exists in many jurisdictions. The US deadline is short (30 days from the stock grant) and missing it cannot be fixed. Your job this week is to *know it exists and route it to your accountant on time*, not to self-execute it.

## Glossary cheat sheet

Keep this open in a tab while you read the lectures.

| Term | Plain English |
|------|---------------|
| **Entity** | The legal "person" that *is* your company — it owns assets, signs contracts, and is taxed separately from you. |
| **Incorporate / form** | The act of creating that legal entity with a government registry. |
| **Limited liability** | The wall between the company's debts/risks and your personal assets. Not absolute; commingling can pierce it. |
| **C-corp** | A US corporation taxed as its own entity; the conventional choice for venture-backed startups. Jurisdiction-specific. |
| **LLC** | Limited Liability Company; flexible and simple, but awkward for traditional equity fundraising. |
| **Founder stock** | The shares founders receive at incorporation, representing the Week 4 equity split. |
| **Restricted stock** | Founder stock the company can buy back if a founder leaves early — the mechanism behind vesting. |
| **Vesting** | Earning your stock over time (commonly 4 years). Protects co-founders from each other. |
| **Cliff** | A period (commonly 1 year) before *any* stock vests; quit before it and you leave with nothing. |
| **83(b) election** | (US) A short-deadline, irreversible tax election on restricted stock. File with an accountant, on time. Jurisdictions have analogues. |
| **IP assignment** | A signed agreement transferring ownership of work (code, designs, content) from the person to the company. |
| **SAFE** | Simple Agreement for Future Equity — money now for stock later, at a future priced round. Not debt. |
| **Convertible note** | Like a SAFE but structured as debt (interest + maturity date). |
| **Valuation cap** | The maximum valuation at which a SAFE/note converts — protects the early investor's ownership. |
| **Post-money** | The company's value *including* the new money — the basis for post-money-SAFE dilution (your Week 8 math). |
| **Due diligence** | The investor's pre-wire investigation of your company, including a legal/IP/cap-table review. |
| **Commingling** | Mixing personal and company money. A liability and tax hazard; avoid with a dedicated account. |
| **NDA** | Non-Disclosure Agreement. Useful occasionally; demanding one too eagerly (e.g., from investors) signals inexperience. |
