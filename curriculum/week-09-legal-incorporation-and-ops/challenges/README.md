# Week 9 — Challenges

The exercises drill the basics — questions, an IP audit, a decision canvas. **The challenge stretches you** into the founder skill those exercises were warming up: looking at your own company the way an investor's lawyer will, finding the problems while they're still cheap, and making a real call about whether you're fundable today.

A challenge is not a worksheet with blanks. It's a realistic founder decision scenario plus your own venture, and it produces something you can put in front of an investor (or a cohort reviewer) and defend. It takes 60–120 minutes and the output is portfolio-grade.

## Index

1. **[Challenge 1 — Pre-seed due-diligence readiness](challenge-01-due-diligence-readiness.md)** — run a full pre-seed due-diligence self-audit on your venture, triage every finding into fix-now / fix-before-raise / advisor, and write the one-paragraph "are we fundable?" verdict you'd have to give a lead investor. (~90 min)

## How the challenge is assessed

Challenges are optional — you can pass the week without them. But the due-diligence self-audit is the single highest-leverage Week 9 skill, and it directly de-risks Week 10's pitch (an investor *will* ask "is this a clean company?"). It's assessed on four things:

| Dimension | What "great" looks like |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| **Completeness** | Every diligence area (corporate, IP, ops/finance, people) is checked against your real situation — nothing skipped because it was uncomfortable. |
| **Honesty** | The audit surfaces the actual gaps, including the embarrassing ones. A spotless audit on a messy company is a fail, not a pass. |
| **Triage quality** | Findings are correctly sorted into fix-now (cheap), fix-before-raise, and `[NEEDS ADVISOR]` — and the cheap ones have owners and dates. |
| **The verdict** | The "are we fundable today?" paragraph is defensible: it names the biggest exposure and what it would take to clear it. |

The bar is not "your company is perfectly clean." Almost no real pre-seed company is. The bar is "**you know exactly where you stand and what the fixes are**" — because the founder who can say that survives diligence, and the one who's surprised by it doesn't.
