# Lecture 2 — Agreements and Operational Hygiene

> **Duration:** ~45 min. **Outcome:** You can list the core early agreements and set up lightweight ops without over-engineering.
>
> **Still education, not legal or tax advice.**

## 1. The core early agreements

A young company needs surprisingly few documents, but the few are essential:

- **Founders' agreement** — roles, decision rights, equity, vesting (your Week 4 draft, papered properly).
- **IP assignment** — every founder and contributor assigns their work to the company (Lecture 1).
- **Advisor agreements** — if you take advisors, define what they do and what (small) equity, if any, they get.
- **Contractor / employee agreements** — anyone you pay should have an agreement that includes IP assignment and confidentiality.
- **NDAs — used sparingly.** Most early conversations (including investor and customer ones) do not need an NDA, and asking for one too eagerly signals inexperience. Reserve them for genuinely sensitive situations.

## 2. Operational hygiene (the short list)

Keep ops minimal but clean:

- **A dedicated business bank account.** Never mix personal and company money — commingling creates tax and liability headaches.
- **Basic bookkeeping.** Track income and expenses from day one; a simple spreadsheet or low-cost tool is enough early.
- **Document storage.** One organized place for the signed agreements, the cap table, and key records.
- **Compliance calendar.** Note any filing or tax deadlines so none slip (your accountant can list them).

Resist the urge to buy a dozen SaaS tools. Early ops is about not creating messes, not about looking like a big company.

## 3. The boundary: you vs. a professional

A useful rule: *do the organizing yourself, get the binding documents from a professional.* You can draft your founders' agreement, assemble your checklist, and keep records; you should have a lawyer paper anything that binds people or money, and an accountant handle entity/tax choices and deadlines. Showing up to those meetings prepared (with a checklist and specific questions) saves money and gets better advice.

## 4. The ops minimum, summarized

```
Form entity (with advisor)
  → Issue founder stock with vesting
  → Everyone signs IP assignment
  → Open business bank account
  → Start basic bookkeeping
  → Store signed docs + cap table in one place
  → Note tax/filing deadlines
```

Everything beyond this is premature until the company is bigger.
