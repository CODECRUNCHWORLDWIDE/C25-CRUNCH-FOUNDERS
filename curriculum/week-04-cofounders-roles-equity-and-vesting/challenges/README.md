# Week 4 — Challenges

The exercises drill the building blocks: a defensible split, a decision-rights table, a vesting schedule. **The challenge stretches you into the situation those building blocks exist for** — a real, messy founder decision where the right answer isn't in a template and you have to *reason and defend* under pressure.

This is a **decision scenario**, not a worksheet. You'll be handed a realistic situation, asked to make calls, and graded on the *quality of your reasoning* — whether your decisions are consistent with the instruments you learned, survive the obvious objections, and pass the "would you say it to their face?" test.

## Index

1. **[Challenge 1 — The departing co-founder](challenge-01-the-departing-cofounder.md)** — a co-founder wants out at month 14, the company finally has traction, and what you wrote down (or didn't) on day one now decides who keeps what. (~75–90 min)

## How the challenge is assessed

Challenges are **optional** — you can pass the week without them. But doing this one puts you measurably ahead, because it's the scenario your whole agreement is *for*, and the reasoning reps here are exactly what a future you (or a future investor in diligence) will lean on.

You're assessed on five things, not on landing a "correct" number:

| Dimension | What "strong" looks like |
|---|---|
| **Correct mechanics** | You apply vesting, the cliff, unvested-return, and the vested-equity question correctly. |
| **Consistency** | Your calls match the instruments and the lean you committed to — no convenient amnesia. |
| **The hard part** | You actually grapple with the *vested* equity and the relationship, not just the easy unvested math. |
| **Survives objections** | Your decision holds up against the three most obvious counter-arguments. |
| **Says-it-to-their-face** | Every term you'd impose is one you could state aloud, to the departing person, without flinching. |

A strong submission is typically 600–1,000 words plus any tables. There is no single right answer — there are well-reasoned answers and convenient ones, and the gap between them is the whole point.
