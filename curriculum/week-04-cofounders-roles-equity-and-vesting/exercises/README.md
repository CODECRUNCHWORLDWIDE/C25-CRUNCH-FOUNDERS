# Week 4 — Exercises

Three worksheets. Each one is a thinking tool, not a quiz — the deliverable is *your reasoning*, written down, about *your* company. They feed directly into the mini-project (the co-founder agreement draft), so do them in order and keep the outputs.

These are **business** worksheets — Markdown templates and canvases you fill in. There is no code this week. The hard part is honesty, not formatting.

## Index

1. **[Exercise 1 — Equity-split reasoning](exercise-01-equity-split-reasoning.md)** — justify two equity splits input-by-input (a given scenario and your real one), and tie yours to your Week 1 control-vs-wealth lean. (~45 min)
2. **[Exercise 2 — Decision-rights matrix](exercise-02-decision-rights-matrix.md)** — a fill-in RACI-style worksheet that assigns a final-call owner and a tiebreak to every decision that actually causes founder fights. (~40 min)
3. **[Exercise 3 — Vesting-schedule template](exercise-03-vesting-schedule-template.md)** — build a real four-year/one-year-cliff vesting schedule for each founder, run three departure scenarios, and add an 83(b) reminder. (~45 min)

## How to work the exercises

- **The number is less important than the reasoning.** A 55/45 you can defend beats a 50/50 you can't. Show your work everywhere.
- **Tie every choice back to your Week 1 control-vs-wealth lean.** If a choice contradicts your lean, either the choice is wrong or your lean has changed — say which.
- **Apply the "would you say it to their face?" test** to every line. If a clause only works because your co-founder didn't read it carefully, it fails.
- **If you are solo:** you still do all three. Run them against (a) your own founder vesting and (b) the terms you'd offer a first key hire. Solo founders need decision rights (for when they hire), vesting, and an 83(b) election too.
- **This is education, not legal or tax advice.** The worksheets make you a sharp client for a real lawyer in Week 9; they don't replace one.
- Commit each completed worksheet to your Week 4 repo. They are the raw material for the mini-project.

## Checklist

Mark the week's exercises done when:

- [ ] **Exercise 1** — both scenarios worked input-by-input; a proposed split with reasoning for each; Scenario B ties to your control-vs-wealth lean.
- [ ] **Exercise 2** — every decision row has a final-call owner; every shared/contested row has a tiebreak rule; no blank cells.
- [ ] **Exercise 3** — a per-founder vesting table with the cliff marked; three departure scenarios computed; the 83(b) 30-day reminder noted.
- [ ] All three committed to your Week 4 repo.
- [ ] Each output is something you could read aloud to your co-founder without flinching.
