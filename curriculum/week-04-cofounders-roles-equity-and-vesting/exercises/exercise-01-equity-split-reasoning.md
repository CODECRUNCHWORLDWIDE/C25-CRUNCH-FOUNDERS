# Exercise 1 — Equity-Split Reasoning

**Goal:** Practice justifying an equity split from its inputs, so your real split (in the mini-project) is a decision, not a reflex.

**Estimated time:** 45 minutes.

---

## What to produce

Create `exercises/exercise-01-equity-split-reasoning.md`. Work two scenarios.

**Scenario A (given):** Founder 1 had the idea, has worked six months full-time, brings customer access, put in $5,000. Founder 2 joins now, full-time, brings the engineering skill, no capital. Propose a split and justify it against each input (idea/prior work, time, capital, skill, opportunity cost). Then state what changes if Founder 2 can only commit part-time.

**Scenario B (yours):** Your real situation (or your closest realistic one). Same input-by-input justification. Then state your split and tie it to your Week 1 control-vs-wealth lean.

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] File present; both scenarios worked input-by-input.
- [ ] A proposed split with reasoning for each.

---

## Hints

- A "fair" split is one both parties understand and accept under stress, not necessarily 50/50.
- If you are solo, treat Scenario B as your founder vesting plus the terms you would offer a first key hire.
