# Week 4 Homework

Three problems, about **2 hours**. Work in your Week 4 repo. Keep adding discovery interviews toward ten.

---

## Problem 1 — Decision-rights table

**Problem statement.** At `notes/decision-rights.md`, build the decision-rights table from Lecture 1 §2 for your venture (real or planned). Assign a final-call owner to each row and a tiebreak rule for any shared ones.

**Acceptance criteria.** File present; at least five decisions; a final-call owner each; no blanks. Committed.

**Hint.** If you are solo, note who you would *want* deciding each once you hire, and where you would keep the final call.

**Estimated time.** 30 min.

---

## Problem 2 — Cliff thought experiment

**Problem statement.** At `notes/cliff-experiment.md`, model two cases for a co-founder who leaves at month three: (a) no vesting, (b) four-year vest with a one-year cliff. State what happens to their stake and to the remaining cap table in each, and who is protected.

**Acceptance criteria.** File present; both cases worked; the protection effect explained. Committed.

**Hint.** Use round numbers (e.g., a 60/40 split) to keep the arithmetic clear.

**Estimated time.** 30 min.

---

## Problem 3 — The unhappy-path clause

**Problem statement.** At `notes/departure-clause.md`, draft in plain language what your agreement says when a founder leaves: treatment of unvested vs. vested equity, any buyback right, and IP/account handover. Flag the parts you would have a lawyer review.

**Acceptance criteria.** File present; unvested, vested, buyback, and IP all addressed; lawyer-review flags noted. Committed.

**Hint.** Write the unhappy path while everyone is happy — it is the only fair time.

**Estimated time.** 45 min.
