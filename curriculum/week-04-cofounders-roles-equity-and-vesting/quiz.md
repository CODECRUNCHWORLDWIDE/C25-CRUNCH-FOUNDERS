# Week 4 — Quiz

Ten questions. Notes closed. Aim for 8/10 before drafting your agreement. Answer key at the bottom — don't peek.

---

**Q1.** Why decide equity and roles early, in writing?

- A) Investors legally require it before you have a product.
- B) Co-founder conflict is a top startup killer, and deferring these talks makes them adversarial once the company has value.
- C) It is legally mandatory before incorporation in every jurisdiction.
- D) It impresses early customers.

---

**Q2.** An automatic 50/50 split between two founders is best described as:

- A) Always correct — it's the only fair split.
- B) Always wrong — someone always contributes more.
- C) Sometimes right, but it should be a reasoned decision, not an avoidance of an awkward conversation.
- D) Illegal in most jurisdictions.

---

**Q3.** You're deciding whether someone should be a **co-founder** or an **early employee**. The sharpest filter from Lecture 1 is:

- A) "Do they have a technical degree?"
- B) "Would I make this person a 50/50 partner in a small business, with my own money on the line, today?"
- C) "Are they willing to work for free?"
- D) "Do they have startup experience?"

---

**Q4.** Which of these is **not** one of the five inputs to an equity split from Lecture 1?

- A) Idea / prior work.
- B) Time commitment, now and going forward.
- C) Which founder is older.
- D) Skill and replaceability.

---

**Q5.** The standard founder vesting shape in 2026 is:

- A) All equity owned outright on day one, forever.
- B) Four-year vest with a one-year cliff, monthly thereafter.
- C) Ten-year vest, no cliff.
- D) Vesting only triggered by an acquisition.

---

**Q6.** A co-founder on a four-year vest with a one-year cliff leaves at **month 11**. They keep:

- A) All of their equity.
- B) About 25%.
- C) Nothing — the cliff has not been reached.
- D) About half.

---

**Q7.** The same co-founder, same schedule, leaves at **month 24**. They have vested:

- A) 0%.
- B) 25%.
- C) 50%.
- D) 100%.

---

**Q8.** Vesting with a cliff primarily protects:

- A) Only the investors.
- B) Everyone — the company and the founders who stay and carry the load.
- C) Only the departing founder.
- D) No one; it's just paperwork distrust.

---

**Q9.** What is an **83(b) election**, and what's the catch?

- A) A vote to elect a board member; it must pass by majority.
- B) A filing that lets a founder pay tax now on near-worthless founder stock instead of later as it appreciates — and it must be filed within **30 days** of the grant, with no extensions.
- C) A type of acceleration that triggers on acquisition.
- D) A clause that gives the company the right to buy back vested shares.

---

**Q10.** **Double-trigger** acceleration means unvested equity accelerates when:

- A) The company is acquired (one event is enough).
- B) The founder is fired for any reason.
- C) *Both* the company is acquired **and** the founder is terminated within a window after.
- D) The company misses two consecutive revenue targets.

---

## Answer key

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answers</summary>

1. **B** — defer these conversations and they turn adversarial once there's value to fight over; A/C/D are false. (L1 §1, §4)
2. **C** — equal can be right, but as a *decision*, not an avoidance. (L1 §3)
3. **B** — the "50/50 partner with my own money" filter separates a true co-founder from an early employee/advisor/contractor. (L1 §1)
4. **C** — age is not an input. The five are idea/prior work, time, capital, skill/replaceability, opportunity cost. (L1 §3)
5. **B** — four-year vest, one-year cliff, monthly thereafter — the 2026 default. (L2 §1)
6. **C** — nothing vests before the one-year cliff. Month 11 = zero. (L2 §1)
7. **C** — 24 of 48 months = 50% vested. (L2 §1)
8. **B** — it's mutual protection, especially for the founders who stay and carry the load; investors require it anyway. (L2 §2)
9. **B** — pay tax now on cheap founder stock; the 30-day deadline is unforgiving and missing it is a costly, common mistake. (L2 §6)
10. **C** — double-trigger requires *both* the acquisition and the termination; single-trigger needs only the acquisition. (L2 §4)

</details>
