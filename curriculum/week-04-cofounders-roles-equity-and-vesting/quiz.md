# Week 4 — Quiz

Six questions. Notes closed. Answer key at the bottom.

---

**Q1.** Why decide equity and roles early, in writing?

- A) Investors require it before you have a product.
- B) Co-founder conflict is a top startup killer, and deferring these talks makes them adversarial once the company has value.
- C) It is legally mandatory before incorporation.
- D) It impresses customers.

**Q2.** An automatic 50/50 split is:

- A) Always correct between two founders.
- B) Always wrong; someone always contributes more.
- C) Sometimes right, but should be a reasoned decision, not an avoidance of an awkward talk.
- D) Illegal in most jurisdictions.

**Q3.** A standard founder vesting shape is:

- A) All equity owned on day one, forever.
- B) Four-year vest with a one-year cliff.
- C) Ten-year vest, no cliff.
- D) Vesting only after an acquisition.

**Q4.** A co-founder leaves at month eleven under a one-year cliff. They keep:

- A) All their equity.
- B) About 25%.
- C) Nothing — the cliff has not been reached.
- D) Half.

**Q5.** Vesting primarily protects:

- A) Only the investors.
- B) Everyone — the company and the founders who stay and carry the load.
- C) Only the departing founder.
- D) No one; it is just paperwork.

**Q6.** A blank cell in your decision-rights table represents:

- A) A decision that doesn't matter.
- B) A future fight with no agreed resolution path.
- C) Something only the board can decide.
- D) A legal requirement.

---

## Answer key

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

1. **B** — defer and it turns adversarial. (L1 §1, §4)
2. **C** — a decision, not a reflex. (L1 §3)
3. **B** — four-year vest, one-year cliff. (L2 §1)
4. **C** — nothing vests before the cliff. (L2 §1)
5. **B** — mutual protection, especially the founders who stay. (L2 §2)
6. **B** — undefined decision rights are future fights. (L1 §2)

</details>
