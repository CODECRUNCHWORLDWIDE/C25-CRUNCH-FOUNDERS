# Mini-Project — The Co-founder Agreement Draft

> Produce a plain-language co-founder agreement draft covering roles, decision rights, the equity split with reasoning, and a vesting schedule with a cliff. Solo founders draft the equivalent: their own founder vesting plus the terms for a first key hire.

**This is a template and an exercise, not legal advice.** When you actually form a company, have a lawyer paper it.

**Estimated time:** ~1.75 hours.

## What you will produce

A file `COFOUNDER-AGREEMENT-DRAFT.md` with these sections:

### 1. Parties and roles
Who the founders are (roles/initials, no sensitive data) and each person's primary responsibilities.

### 2. Decision rights
The table from the homework: each major decision and its final-call owner, plus tiebreak rules.

### 3. Equity split
The split and the input-by-input reasoning (from Exercise 1). State explicitly how it reflects your control-vs-wealth lean.

### 4. Vesting
The schedule (e.g., four-year vest, one-year cliff), stated for every founder, with the simple chart or a sentence describing it.

### 5. Departure terms
Treatment of unvested vs. vested equity, any buyback right, IP/account handover.

### 6. Open questions for a lawyer
A short list of everything you would want a professional to review before signing.

---

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] File at `mini-project/COFOUNDER-AGREEMENT-DRAFT.md`.
- [ ] All six sections present.
- [ ] Decision-rights table has no blanks.
- [ ] Equity split includes reasoning and a control/wealth tie-in.
- [ ] Vesting includes a cliff and applies to all founders.
- [ ] A "for a lawyer" section exists.
- [ ] A visible note that this is not legal advice.
- [ ] Committed.

## Rubric

| Criterion | Weight |
|-----------|------:|
| Decision rights leave no gaps | 25% |
| Equity reasoning is sound and tied to the control/wealth lean | 25% |
| Vesting includes a cliff and covers everyone | 25% |
| Departure terms handle the unhappy path | 15% |
| Lawyer-review questions are identified | 10% |
