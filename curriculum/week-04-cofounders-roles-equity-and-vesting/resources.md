# Week 4 — Resources

Every resource on this page is **free** and publicly accessible without a paywall. Where a tool has a paid tier, the free tier or the free educational content is what we link. **Everything here is education, not legal or tax advice.** The goal is to make you a sharp client for a real lawyer in Week 9 — not to replace one.

## The founder's-dilemmas core idea (skim, then keep)

The empirical backbone of this week is Noam Wasserman's research: most founder problems are *people* problems, decided early, and many are predictable. You do not need the whole book, but you should recognize the framing.

- **Noam Wasserman — "The Founder's Dilemmas" overview / interviews** (HBS Working Knowledge has free summaries; search "Wasserman founder's dilemmas HBS"):
  <https://www.hbs.edu/faculty/Pages/profile.aspx?facId=190068>
- **First Round Review — "The Equity Equation" / co-founder equity pieces** (free, practitioner-grade):
  <https://review.firstround.com/>

## Glossary cheat sheet

Keep this open in a tab while you draft.

| Term | Plain English |
|------|---------------|
| **Co-founder** | A partner who shares ownership and the hardest early decisions — not just an early employee. |
| **Decision rights** | Who has the *final call* on a given decision when founders disagree. Distinct from titles. |
| **Equity split** | How founder ownership is divided. A decision, not a default. |
| **Vesting** | Earning your equity over time (commonly four years) instead of owning it all on day one. |
| **Cliff** | A period (commonly one year) before *any* equity vests; at the cliff a chunk vests at once. |
| **Restricted stock (RSA)** | Shares a founder buys/receives up front that are subject to repurchase if they leave before vesting. The usual founder instrument. |
| **83(b) election** | A tax filing (within **30 days** of the stock grant) that lets a founder pay tax on the low early value. Missing it is a costly, common mistake. |
| **Acceleration** | Equity vesting faster on a trigger — single-trigger (acquisition) or double-trigger (acquisition *and* termination). |
| **Good leaver / bad leaver** | Whether a departing founder keeps or forfeits vested equity depends on *how* they leave. |
| **Buyback / repurchase right** | The company's right to buy back a departing founder's shares, often unvested at cost and sometimes vested at a set price. |
| **Cap table** | The record of who owns what — every founder, option, and (later) investor. |
| **Dilution** | The reduction in your ownership *percentage* when new shares are issued (e.g., in a fundraise). Affects all founders together. |
| **IP assignment** | The agreement that work product belongs to the company, not the individual. Covered in depth in Week 9. |
