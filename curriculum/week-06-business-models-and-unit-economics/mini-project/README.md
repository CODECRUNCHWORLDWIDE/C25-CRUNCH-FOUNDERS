# Mini-Project — Lean Canvas + Unit-Economics Sheet

> Produce a one-page lean business model canvas and a unit-economics spreadsheet for your idea, with assumptions stated and one sensitivity case. Both are capstone deliverables.

**Estimated time:** ~1.75 hours.

## What you will produce

1. **`canvas/CANVAS.md`** — the nine-block canvas (or lean variant), with the weakest block flagged as your riskiest business-model assumption.
2. **A unit-economics sheet** — a real spreadsheet (Google Sheets / LibreOffice; export a CSV or screenshot into `unit-economics/`) computing:
   - Price and contribution margin (variable costs itemized).
   - CAC (including founder-led sales).
   - LTV (contribution-based) and lifetime assumption.
   - LTV:CAC ratio.
   - Payback period.
   - One sensitivity case (e.g., CAC doubles), recomputed.
3. **`unit-economics/VERDICT.md`** — the honest verdict: can it make money at scale, and what must be true? Name the most fragile assumption.

---

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] Canvas with all blocks filled and weakest block flagged.
- [ ] Spreadsheet computing contribution margin, CAC, LTV, LTV:CAC, payback.
- [ ] Every input has a labeled assumption.
- [ ] At least one sensitivity case recomputed.
- [ ] A written verdict naming the most fragile assumption.
- [ ] Committed (export/screenshot the sheet into the repo).

## Rubric

| Criterion | Weight |
|-----------|------:|
| Canvas is complete and internally consistent | 25% |
| All five unit-economics metrics computed correctly | 30% |
| Assumptions are explicit and reasonable | 20% |
| Sensitivity case is included | 15% |
| Verdict is honest and names the fragile assumption | 10% |
