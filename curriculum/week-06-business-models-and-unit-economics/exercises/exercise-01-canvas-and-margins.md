# Exercise 1 — Canvas and Margins

**Goal:** Fill the business model canvas for your idea and compute contribution margin, the foundation of the unit-economics sheet.

**Estimated time:** 50 minutes.

---

## What to produce

Create `exercises/exercise-01-canvas-and-margins.md`:

1. **Canvas** — all nine blocks filled (or the lean variant). Mark the one block you are least confident about as your riskiest business-model assumption.
2. **Revenue model + price** — which model, what starting price, value-based or cost-plus reasoning.
3. **Contribution margin** — price minus per-customer variable cost, with each variable cost itemized and assumed.

```markdown
# Canvas & margins — <your idea>

## Canvas
- Customer segments:
- Value propositions:
- Channels:
- Customer relationships:
- Revenue streams:
- Key resources:
- Key activities:
- Key partners:
- Cost structure:
Weakest block (riskiest assumption):

## Revenue model & price
Model: ___  ·  Price: ___  ·  Reasoning:

## Contribution margin
Price: ___
- Variable cost 1: ___ (assumption)
- Variable cost 2: ___ (assumption)
Contribution margin = ___
```

---

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] File present; all nine blocks filled; weakest block flagged.
- [ ] Revenue model and price chosen with reasoning.
- [ ] Contribution margin computed with itemized, assumed variable costs.
- [ ] Committed.

---

## Hints

- If a block is empty, that emptiness is information — it is your next test.
- When unsure on price, write the higher number and note you will test it.
