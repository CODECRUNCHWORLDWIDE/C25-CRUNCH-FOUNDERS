# Week 6 — Quiz

Six questions. Notes closed. Answer key at the bottom.

---

**Q1.** The business model canvas is best treated as:

- A) A finished plan you execute unchanged.
- B) A one-page hypothesis where each block is a guess to be tested.
- C) A legal document.
- D) A pitch deck.

**Q2.** Contribution margin is:

- A) Price plus fixed costs.
- B) Price minus variable cost per unit.
- C) Total revenue minus total costs.
- D) The same as CAC.

**Q3.** CAC is computed as:

- A) Variable cost per customer.
- B) Total sales & marketing spend ÷ customers acquired.
- C) Price ÷ lifetime.
- D) Revenue ÷ churn.

**Q4.** LTV should be based on:

- A) Revenue per customer.
- B) Contribution margin per period × expected lifetime.
- C) The price tag alone.
- D) CAC.

**Q5.** An LTV:CAC ratio well below 1 means:

- A) Scale aggressively; growth fixes it.
- B) You lose money on each customer — do not pour fuel on it.
- C) The product is too cheap to matter.
- D) Payback is automatically short.

**Q6.** Why run a sensitivity check (e.g., "what if CAC doubles")?

- A) To make the spreadsheet longer.
- B) Because a model that only works under best-case assumptions is a wish, not a model.
- C) It is legally required.
- D) Investors dislike it.

---

## Answer key

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

1. **B** — a testable hypothesis. (L1 §1)
2. **B** — price minus variable cost. (L2 §1)
3. **B** — spend ÷ customers acquired. (L2 §2)
4. **B** — contribution × lifetime, not revenue. (L2 §3)
5. **B** — losing money per customer; don't scale. (L2 §4)
6. **B** — stress-test the assumptions. (L2 §6)

</details>
