# Week 6 — Challenges

The exercises drill *your own* model. **Challenges stretch you** by dropping you into a realistic founder decision where the numbers are already broken and you have to diagnose and fix them under constraints — the way you'll have to in a board meeting or an investor's office. Each takes 60–120 minutes and produces a written deliverable you can put in your portfolio (and reuse the reasoning for in Week 7's GTM and Week 10's pitch).

## Index

1. **[Challenge 1 — The leaky-model turnaround](challenge-01-the-leaky-model-turnaround.md)** — you inherit a fast-growing startup whose unit economics are upside-down (CAC > LTV). Diagnose what's broken, propose the *one* highest-leverage fix, and rebuild the model to prove it works — or argue honestly that it can't be saved. (~90 min)

Challenges are **optional**. You can pass the week without them. But the diagnostic muscle here — looking at a model and instantly seeing which lever is bent — is exactly what separates a founder who survives a hard investor question from one who freezes. The "leaky model" reasoning reappears directly in **Week 7** (when you read your real funnel) and **Week 10** (when an investor stress-tests your pitch live).

## How challenges are assessed

Unlike the exercises (which are about *your* concept and have no single right answer), challenges have a scenario with embedded numbers, so they can be graded against a rubric. You're assessed on:

| Dimension | What "great" looks like |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| **Diagnosis** | You correctly identify *which* lever is broken (price / contribution margin / CAC / retention), with the math to prove it — not a vibe. |
| **Prioritization** | You name the *single* highest-leverage fix and justify why it beats the alternatives, rather than listing ten things. |
| **Rebuilt model** | Your post-fix unit economics are computed correctly and internally consistent, with every assumption stated. |
| **Honesty** | You run a sensitivity check on your fix and admit what would have to be true. If the model genuinely can't be saved, you say so — "kill it" is a valid, gradeable answer when the math demands it. |
| **Communication** | A reader who isn't a finance person understands your diagnosis and recommendation in two minutes. |

Each challenge file states its own specific acceptance criteria and rubric weights. Submit your write-up as a Markdown file in your Week 6 repo under `challenges/`.
