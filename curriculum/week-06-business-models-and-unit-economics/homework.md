# Week 6 Homework

Three problems, about **2 hours**. Work in your Week 6 repo. Keep interviewing toward ten.

---

## Problem 1 — Revenue-model comparison

**Problem statement.** At `notes/revenue-models.md`, evaluate two revenue models for your idea (e.g., subscription vs. usage). For each: how customers experience it, what it does to predictability, and which you choose and why.

**Acceptance criteria.** File present; two models compared; a choice with reasoning. Committed.

**Hint.** Match the model to how the customer actually gets value over time.

**Estimated time.** 30 min.

---

## Problem 2 — Full unit-economics math

**Problem statement.** At `notes/unit-economics.md`, compute contribution margin, CAC, LTV, LTV:CAC, and payback for your idea, with every assumption labeled. Then run one sensitivity case (e.g., CAC doubles) and recompute.

**Acceptance criteria.** File present; all five metrics computed; assumptions labeled; one sensitivity case. Committed.

**Hint.** Include founder-led sales effort in CAC. Use contribution, not revenue, in LTV.

**Estimated time.** 50 min.

---

## Problem 3 — The honest verdict

**Problem statement.** At `notes/model-verdict.md`, answer in 150–250 words: can this model make money at scale, and what would have to be true? Name the single most fragile assumption.

**Acceptance criteria.** File present; 150–250 words; a clear verdict; the most fragile assumption named. Committed.

**Hint.** "It works if CAC stays under $X and customers stay Y months" is a good shape.

**Estimated time.** 30 min.
