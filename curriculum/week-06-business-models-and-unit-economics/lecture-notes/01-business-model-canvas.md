# Lecture 1 — The Business Model Canvas

> **Duration:** ~1 hour. **Outcome:** You can fill the nine-block canvas as a hypothesis and choose a revenue model and starting price.

## 1. The canvas as a one-page hypothesis

The business model canvas describes how a business creates, delivers, and captures value, on one page. It is a *hypothesis*, not a plan — each block is a guess to be tested. The nine blocks:

```
┌───────────────┬───────────────┬───────────────────┬───────────────┬───────────────┐
│ Key Partners  │ Key Activities│ Value             │ Customer      │ Customer      │
│               │               │ Propositions      │ Relationships │ Segments      │
│               ├───────────────┤                   ├───────────────┤               │
│               │ Key Resources │                   │ Channels      │               │
├───────────────┴───────────────┴─────────┬─────────┴───────────────┴───────────────┤
│ Cost Structure                          │ Revenue Streams                          │
└─────────────────────────────────────────┴──────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

- **Customer segments** — who you serve (be specific; your Week 3 segment).
- **Value propositions** — the painkiller you deliver.
- **Channels** — how you reach and deliver (links to Week 7).
- **Customer relationships** — self-serve, high-touch, community.
- **Revenue streams** — how you get paid.
- **Key resources / activities / partners** — what it takes to deliver.
- **Cost structure** — what it costs.

A "lean canvas" variant swaps some blocks for problem, solution, key metrics, and unfair advantage — use whichever shape your cohort prefers; the discipline is the same.

## 2. Revenue models

- **One-time sale** — pay once. Simple; no recurring revenue.
- **Subscription** — recurring monthly/annual. Predictable; the dominant software model.
- **Transaction / marketplace take rate** — a cut of each transaction.
- **Usage-based** — pay for what you consume.
- **Advertising** — free to users, monetize attention (needs scale).
- **Freemium** — free tier funnels into paid; watch conversion.

Pick the one your customers already understand and that matches how they get value.

## 3. Pricing basics

- **Value-based** — price against the value delivered (e.g., money the customer saves). Usually the right frame.
- **Cost-plus** — price your costs plus a margin. Easy, but leaves value on the table.
- **The underpricing trap** — first-timers price too low out of fear. Low price signals low value and starves the unit economics. When unsure, test a higher number.

Pricing is a hypothesis too; you will test it in discovery/validation and adjust.

## 4. Find the weak block

The block you cannot fill confidently is your riskiest business-model assumption. Flag it — it may be the next thing to test after this week.
