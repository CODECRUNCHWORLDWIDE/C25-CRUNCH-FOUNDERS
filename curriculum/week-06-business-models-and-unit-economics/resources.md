# Week 6 — Resources

Every resource on this page is **free** to read or use at the level we need it. Strategyzer, Lean Stack, Y Combinator's library, a16z, and First Round Review publish their core frameworks openly. No paywalled books are required — where we cite a book, the idea it contains is summarized in a free article we link instead. If a link 404s, please open an issue so we can replace it.

A note on sources: business-model and metrics writing ages fast and is full of confident nonsense. We've biased toward (a) the people who *invented* the framework, (b) operators publishing real numbers, and (c) the handful of essays that have survived a decade of scrutiny. Be skeptical of any "industry benchmark" with no methodology attached — including the rules of thumb we teach.

## Glossary cheat sheet

Keep this open in a tab. These are the terms you will use all week and all of Week 7.

| Term | Plain English |
|------|---------------|
| **Business model** | How the whole thing creates, delivers, and captures value. The nine boxes. |
| **Revenue model** | *How* you charge: subscription, transaction, usage, ads, etc. One box inside the business model. |
| **Pricing model** | The specific numbers and structure: $99/mo, $0.002/call, 15% take rate. |
| **Contribution margin** | Price minus the *variable* cost to serve one more unit/customer. The profit per unit before fixed costs. |
| **Gross margin** | Revenue minus cost of goods sold, as a %. Related to but not the same as contribution margin. |
| **Variable cost** | A cost that scales with each unit/customer (payment fees, hosting per user, SMS). |
| **Fixed cost** | A cost that doesn't move with one more customer (rent, salaries). Excluded from unit economics on purpose. |
| **CAC** | Customer Acquisition Cost — fully-loaded spend to land one paying customer (incl. founder time). |
| **Blended CAC** | CAC across *all* customers including organic. Flattering; use with care. |
| **Paid CAC** | CAC for customers from paid channels only. The number that matters as you scale. |
| **LTV / CLV** | Lifetime Value — total *contribution* a customer generates before they churn. |
| **Churn** | The rate at which customers leave. The inverse of retention. Drives lifetime. |
| **Retention** | The share of customers who stay. High retention = long lifetime = high LTV. |
| **LTV:CAC** | The ratio of what a customer is worth to what they cost. Rule of thumb: ≈3:1 or better. |
| **CAC payback** | Months for a customer's contribution to repay their CAC. Rule of thumb: under ~12 months. |
| **Take rate** | The % a marketplace keeps from each transaction (e.g., 15%). |
| **Freemium** | A free tier funnels users toward a paid tier. A funnel, not a price. |
| **Net revenue retention (NRR)** | Revenue from a cohort over time including expansion, net of churn. >100% means you grow without new customers. |
| **Sensitivity** | How the model changes when an assumption moves (CAC doubles, retention halves). |
