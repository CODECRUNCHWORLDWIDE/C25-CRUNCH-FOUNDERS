# Week 1 — Resources

Every resource on this page is **free** or has a substantial free version. Essays are public. Wikipedia is free. The books listed have free chapters, free summaries, or are commonly available through libraries — we link the free entry point, not a paywall. Read narrowly: pick two or three things from this list and actually read them, rather than skimming all of it.

## Required reading (work it into your week)

- **Paul Graham — "How to Get Startup Ideas"** — the canonical essay on problem-first ideation; the source of "live in the future and build what's missing":
  <https://paulgraham.com/startupideas.html>
- **Paul Graham — "Do Things That Don't Scale"** — why early founders should do unscalable, manual things; reframes the founder's real job:
  <https://paulgraham.com/ds.html>
- **Y Combinator — Startup School library** — free, current founder curriculum; start with "How to Get Startup Ideas" and "How to Evaluate Startup Ideas":
  <https://www.startupschool.org/>
- **Marc Andreessen — "The Only Thing That Matters"** — the original product-market-fit essay; read it so you know what founder-market fit is the precursor to:
  <https://pmarchive.com/guide_to_startups_part4.html>

## The control-vs-wealth research

The "rich vs. king" framing in Lecture 2 is not folklore — it comes from a decade of founder data.

- **Noam Wasserman — "The Founder's Dilemma" (HBR, free)** — the short, free entry point to the research behind rich-vs-king, equity splits, and the people decisions:
  <https://hbr.org/2008/02/the-founders-dilemma>
- **Noam Wasserman — "The Founder's Dilemmas" (book)** — the full treatment; the source for Week 4's equity material too. Commonly available through libraries.

## Glossary cheat sheet

Keep this open in a tab.

| Term | Plain English |
|------|---------------|
| **Founder-market fit** | Whether *you, specifically* are well-suited to *this* problem and market. Checkable before you build. |
| **Standing** | Your honest right to work on a problem — "why you, why now?" Lived pain and access, not interest. |
| **Unfair advantage** | Something hard for others to copy: lived experience, domain access, distribution, skill, or obsession. |
| **Control vs. wealth** | The "rich vs. king" trade. You usually cannot maximize both ownership/decision-rights and financial upside. |
| **Painkiller vs. vitamin** | Painkiller = urgent, frequent, expensive problem people already work around. Vitamin = nice-to-have. |
| **Discovery** | Talking to real people to find real problems, before building. The opposite of inspiration-first. |
| **Vanity metric** | A number that flatters (signups, page views) but doesn't predict the business (usage, revenue, retention). |
| **Pre-seed** | The earliest priced/SAFE round, typically before meaningful revenue. (Detail comes in Week 8.) |
| **SAFE** | Simple Agreement for Future Equity — the standard early fundraising instrument since the mid-2010s. (Week 8.) |
| **Pivot** | A structured change in strategy (problem, customer, or solution) based on what you learned. (Week 5.) |
| **Pre-mortem** | Imagining the project has already failed and working backward to the causes — a planning tool, not pessimism. |
| **Founder brief** | This week's deliverable: your advantages, your control lean, and a problem space you have standing in. |
