# Week 1 Homework

Four practice problems revisiting the week's topics. The full set takes about **2 hours** — shorter than later weeks, because Week 1 is the mindset week. Work in your Week 1 repository so each problem produces at least one commit.

Each problem includes a **problem statement**, **acceptance criteria**, a **hint**, and an **estimated time**.

---

## Problem 1 — Founder teardown

**Problem statement.** Pick one founder you can read freely about online (a public YC Startup School talk, a Paul Graham essay, a Wikipedia biography). Write a short teardown at `notes/founder-teardown.md`:

- Who they are and what they built (two sentences).
- Their apparent **founder-market fit** — what unfair advantages did they have?
- Their apparent **control-vs-wealth lean**, with the evidence you inferred it from.
- One decision they made that you would make differently, and why.

**Acceptance criteria.**

- File at `notes/founder-teardown.md`, all four bullets present.
- The source is free and the link works.
- Committed.

**Hint.** Founder-market fit is easiest to spot in founders who built for an industry they came from. Start at <https://www.startupschool.org/> or <https://www.paulgraham.com/articles.html>.

**Estimated time.** 30 minutes.

---

## Problem 2 — Painkiller vs. vitamin sort

**Problem statement.** List six problems you have personally felt in the last month — anything, work or life. At `notes/painkiller-sort.md`, sort each into **painkiller** or **vitamin** and justify in one sentence using the urgent/recurring/costly test.

**Acceptance criteria.**

- File at `notes/painkiller-sort.md`.
- Six problems, each labeled and justified.
- At least one is honestly labeled a vitamin (most things are).
- Committed.

**Hint.** Be ruthless. "It would be nice if..." is a vitamin. "I lose two hours every week to..." is a painkiller.

**Estimated time.** 25 minutes.

---

## Problem 3 — The standing test

**Problem statement.** Take the problem space from your founder brief. At `notes/standing-test.md`, answer "why me, why now" in detail, then list three concrete people you could interview about it in the next two weeks (first names or roles only — no private info).

**Acceptance criteria.**

- File at `notes/standing-test.md`.
- A detailed "why me, why now" (not one line).
- Three reachable interview targets named by role.
- An honest note if you *cannot* name three — that is a finding about your standing.
- Committed.

**Hint.** If you cannot name three reachable people, your standing in this space may be weaker than it felt. That is exactly the kind of thing Week 1 is for catching.

**Estimated time.** 25 minutes.

---

## Problem 4 — Week 1 reflection

**Problem statement.** Write a 250-word reflection at `notes/week-01-reflection.md`:

1. Which idea from Lecture 1 or 2 most changed how you see the founder's job?
2. Did your control-vs-wealth lean survive Exercise 2's three scenarios, or did it flip?
3. One belief about startups you held on Monday that you now doubt.

**Acceptance criteria.**

- File at `notes/week-01-reflection.md`, 200–300 words, each question in its own paragraph.
- Committed.

**Hint.** This one is yours, not for a grader. Be honest — future-you will reread it after Week 10.

**Estimated time.** 20 minutes.
