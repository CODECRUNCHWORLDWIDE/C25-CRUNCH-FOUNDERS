# Exercise 2 — Control vs. Wealth, Made Concrete

**Goal:** Move your control-vs-wealth lean from a vague feeling to a tested position by running it against three concrete decisions you will actually face.

**Estimated time:** 30 minutes.

---

## Why this exercise exists

It is easy to say "I want both." The trade only becomes real when a specific decision forces a choice. This exercise puts you in front of three such decisions now, so the answer is not improvised later under pressure.

---

## What to produce

Create `exercises/exercise-02-control-vs-wealth.md`. State your lean at the top, then answer each scenario with the choice *and* the cost you accept.

---

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] File at `exercises/exercise-02-control-vs-wealth.md`.
- [ ] A stated default lean with a reason.
- [ ] All three scenarios answered with both a choice and an accepted cost.
- [ ] A closing reflection on whether the lean held up.
- [ ] Committed.

---

## Hints

- There is no graded "right" answer. A consistent control lean and a consistent wealth lean are both fine; an *inconsistent* one is the useful finding.
- If you chose "control" up top but gave away control in all three scenarios, your real lean is wealth. Write that down — it will change how you approach Weeks 4 and 8.
