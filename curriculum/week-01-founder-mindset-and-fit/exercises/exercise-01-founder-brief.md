# Exercise 1 — Founder Brief

**Goal:** Write a one-page brief of who *you* are as a founder. Unfair advantages, control-vs-wealth lean, a problem space you have standing in, and what (if anything) you need from a co-founder. This brief travels with you through the whole course and anchors the Week 1 mini-project.

**Estimated time:** 45 minutes.

---

## Why this exercise exists

Most first-time founders pick a problem because it is interesting, then discover halfway in that they have no access to customers and no real stamina for it. A pre-written brief forces the honesty up front: it makes you state your advantages, your trade-off lean, and your standing *before* you fall in love with an idea in Week 2.

---

## What to produce

Create `exercises/exercise-01-founder-brief.md` in your Week 1 repo with the headings below. One to three honest sentences per section. No essays. One printed page (~400 words).

```markdown
# Founder brief — <your name>

## My unfair advantages
(One to three. Be specific. "I worked three years in dental-office operations" beats "I understand healthcare.")

## What I am missing
(Honesty. "I cannot build software." "I have no network in this industry." "I have never sold anything.")

## My control-vs-wealth lean
(Pick a default: more toward CONTROL or more toward WEALTH. Two sentences on why.)

## A problem space I have standing in
(One space. Then the one-sentence "why me, why now.")

## Solo or co-founders?
(Your current lean, and IF co-founders: the one specific thing you need that you do not have.)

## My obsession test
(Will I still care about this problem in three years when it is boring? One honest sentence.)

## What I want to get out of this course
(One sentence. Not "get rich." A concrete learning or decision goal.)
```

---

## Worked example (use for shape, not content)

```markdown
# Founder brief — Maya

## My unfair advantages
- Four years as an office manager at a busy dental practice; I lived the scheduling chaos daily.
- ~15 warm contacts still working in dental offices I can interview this week.
- I can build a clickable prototype in Figma, though not production software.

## What I am missing
- I cannot ship production code. I will need a technical co-founder or a no-code path for the MVP.
- I have never raised money and do not know how cap tables work yet.

## My control-vs-wealth lean
More toward CONTROL. I would rather own the decisions and grow slower than raise a large round and answer to a board early. I will revisit this in Week 8.

## A problem space I have standing in
Scheduling and patient no-shows for small dental practices. Why me, why now: I lived the pain, I can reach the customers cheaply, and post-pandemic staffing shortages made it worse.

## Solo or co-founders?
Leaning toward one technical co-founder. The specific thing I need: someone who can build and run a real web app, not just prototype it.

## My obsession test
Yes — I was still ranting about no-shows two years after I left the job. That is a good sign.

## What I want to get out of this course
A go/no-go decision on this idea backed by real interviews, not my own certainty.
```

Notice every section names *specifics*. There is no "passionate," no "visionary." Those words mean nothing on a founder brief.

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] File at `exercises/exercise-01-founder-brief.md` in your Week 1 repo.
- [ ] All seven sections present and filled in.
- [ ] No section longer than three sentences.
- [ ] At least one honest "what I am missing."
- [ ] The control-vs-wealth lean is chosen (not "both") with a reason.
- [ ] The problem-space section includes a one-sentence "why me, why now."
- [ ] Fits on one printed page (~400 words).
- [ ] Committed.

---

## Hints

- **Do not inflate your advantages.** "I find this interesting" is not an advantage; lived experience, access, and distribution are.
- **Pick a control-vs-wealth lean even if it feels premature.** You can change it; you cannot skip it.
- **Share it with one trusted person.** Ask: "Does this sound like me, and is the standing claim real?"
