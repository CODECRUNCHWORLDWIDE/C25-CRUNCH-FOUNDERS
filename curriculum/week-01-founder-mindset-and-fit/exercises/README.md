# Week 1 — Exercises

Short, focused worksheets. Each one should take 30–60 minutes. Do them **in order** — the second and third both build on the first. These are not coding exercises (this is a founders course); they are written worksheets, canvases, and templates you fill in honestly and commit to your repo.

These exercises produce the raw material for the week's mini-project (your Founder Operating Document). Nothing you write here is throwaway — you will reference all three at later decisions in the course, so write what is *true*, not what sounds good.

## Index

1. **[Exercise 1 — Founder brief](exercise-01-founder-brief.md)** — write the one-page brief that names your unfair advantages, your control-vs-wealth lean, and a problem space you have standing in. This is the anchor for everything else. (~45 min)
2. **[Exercise 2 — Control vs. wealth, made concrete](exercise-02-control-vs-wealth.md)** — place yourself on the control-vs-wealth line, then test that lean against three concrete decisions you will actually face. (~30 min)
3. **[Exercise 3 — Founder-market-fit canvas](exercise-03-founder-market-fit-canvas.md)** — score yourself on the five unfair advantages across three candidate problem spaces, and pick one to carry into Week 2. (~45 min)

## How to work the exercises

- **Read the prompt, then write your own answer.** Do not paste a template and lightly edit it — type it. The friction is the point; it forces you to actually decide.
- **Use the C25 voice** — problem-first, evidence over adjectives, honest about trade-offs. Ban the following words from every deliverable: *disruptor, visionary, rockstar, ninja, 10x, passionate, game-changer*. They mean nothing and they hide the absence of specifics.
- **Anchor every important claim to the evidence ladder** from Lecture 1: (a) lived it, (b) talked to N people, (c) read it, (d) assumed it. A brief full of "(d) assumed it" claims is a to-do list for Week 3, and that's fine — just label it honestly.
- **Commit each completed exercise to your Week 1 repo as you go.** Small commits, sensible messages. The course treats your repo as the record of your thinking.
- If you get stuck for more than 10 minutes, re-read the relevant lecture section rather than staring at the page.

## Checklist

Mark the week's exercises done when:

- [ ] **Exercise 1** — `exercises/exercise-01-founder-brief.md` exists, all sections filled, a single control-vs-wealth lean chosen (not "both"), at least one honest "what I'm missing," and a "why me, why now" sentence. Committed.
- [ ] **Exercise 2** — `exercises/exercise-02-control-vs-wealth.md` exists, a stated lean, all three scenarios answered with both a *choice* and an *accepted cost*, plus a closing reflection on whether the lean held. Committed.
- [ ] **Exercise 3** — `exercises/exercise-03-founder-market-fit-canvas.md` exists, three problem spaces scored across all five advantages, one space chosen with reasoning, and an honest note on the weakest advantage of the chosen space. Committed.
- [ ] All three use the banned-words rule and anchor key claims to the evidence ladder.
- [ ] The chosen problem space is *consistent* across Exercises 1 and 3 (or you've written a sentence explaining why it changed).

There is no single right answer to any of these. They are deliverables you will reference at every later decision. Take them seriously — a thin, flattering brief produces a thin, flattering company.
