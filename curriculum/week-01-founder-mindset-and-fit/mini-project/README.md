# Mini-Project — Your Founder Operating Document

> Build a single, durable document that captures who you are as a founder: your unfair advantages, your control-vs-wealth lean, your standing in a problem space, your co-founder needs, and your decision principles. You will update it after every major decision in this course and carry it into whatever you build next.

This is the Week 1 deliverable that is meant to outlive the week. The founder brief (Exercise 1) and the control-vs-wealth analysis (Exercise 2) collapse into the **Founder Operating Document** (FOD). One file. One source of truth.

**Estimated time:** ~1.75 hours (split across Friday and Sunday in the suggested schedule).

---

## What you will produce

A single Markdown file, `FOUNDER-OPERATING-DOC.md`, committed to your Week 1 repo *and* copied into your personal portfolio repo (you will keep updating it from there). Roughly 600–900 words. Not an essay — a document of decisions.

---

## Required sections

Each section below must appear, in this order, with the heading text shown.

### 1. Who I am as a founder
Two or three short paragraphs: your unfair advantages, what you are missing, and your obsession test (will you still care in three years?).

### 2. My control-vs-wealth lean
One paragraph stating your default and *why*, plus one line on which scenario from Exercise 2 most tested it.

### 3. My problem space and standing
The space you are entering this course with, and your "why me, why now."

### 4. My co-founder posture
Solo or co-founders, and if co-founders, the specific gaps you need filled. (You will revisit this hard in Week 4.)

### 5. My decision principles
Three to five short rules you commit to, e.g.:
- "I will not build before I have talked to ten people with the problem."
- "I will decide equity and vesting in writing before any money moves."
- "I will not chase signups when I should be measuring usage."

### 6. My anti-patterns to watch
The founder self-deceptions from Lecture 1 §5 that *you specifically* are most at risk of, and how you will catch yourself.

### 7. Version log

| Date | What changed | Trigger |
|------|--------------|---------|
| (today) | First draft | C25 Week 1 |
| ... | ... | ... |

Every major decision in this course should produce a line here.

## Rules

- **You may** reuse text from Exercises 1 and 2 — they were always going to feed in here.
- **You may** keep this in your portfolio repo, but commit a copy under Week 1 for grading.
- **You will not** make this a pitch. No "visionary," no "disruptor," no "10x." Plain language only.

## Rubric

| Criterion | Weight | What "great" looks like |
|-----------|-------:|-------------------------|
| Specificity | 25% | Every section names real choices, not platitudes |
| Honesty | 25% | The "what I am missing" and anti-patterns feel true |
| Lean is decided | 20% | Section 2 commits to control or wealth with a reason |
| Principles are actionable | 20% | A stranger could hold you to Section 5 |
| Version log present | 10% | Section 7 exists with the first entry |
