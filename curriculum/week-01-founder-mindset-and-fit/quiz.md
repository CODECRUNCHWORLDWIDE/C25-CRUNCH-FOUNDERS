# Week 1 — Quiz

Five multiple-choice questions. Take it with your lecture notes closed. Aim for 5/5 before Week 2. Answer key at the bottom — don't peek.

---

**Q1.** According to Lecture 1, what is the founder's *real* job?

- A) Having a brilliant, unique idea no one has thought of.
- B) Deciding what is worth building and for whom, and surviving the people decisions around it.
- C) Raising as much money as possible, as early as possible.
- D) Building the product alone in secret until it is perfect.

---

**Q2.** The **control-vs-wealth tension** says that:

- A) Founders should always choose wealth, because money buys control later.
- B) You can fully maximize both control and wealth at the same time if you work hard enough.
- C) Many founder decisions trade control against wealth, and the mistake is refusing to decide your default lean.
- D) Control only matters for founders who refuse outside investment.

---

**Q3.** Which of the following best describes a **painkiller** problem (vs. a vitamin)?

- A) A mild, occasional annoyance that is easy to ignore.
- B) A problem the founder finds intellectually interesting.
- C) An urgent, recurring problem that already costs people time, money, or pain — they are hacking workarounds.
- D) Any problem that involves new technology.

---

**Q4.** A founder claims strong **founder-market fit**. Which is the *weakest* supporting evidence?

- A) They personally had the problem for years.
- B) They can reach a hundred potential customers this week through existing relationships.
- C) They read an article about the space and found it fascinating.
- D) They have an audience or distribution channel in that market.

---

**Q5.** What does it mean to have **standing** in a problem space?

- A) You were the first person ever to notice the problem.
- B) You have a credible answer to "why you, and why now" — usually lived experience, access, or distribution.
- C) You have already raised money to work on it.
- D) You find the problem more interesting than other problems.

---

## Answer key

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answers</summary>

1. **B** — The idea is the cheap part; the job is deciding what to build, for whom, and surviving the people decisions. (Lecture 1, §1–2)
2. **C** — The two often pull apart; the error is drifting without deciding a default lean. (Lecture 2, §1)
3. **C** — Painkillers are urgent, recurring, and already costly; people pay to make pain stop. (Lecture 1, §4)
4. **C** — Interest is not an advantage. Lived experience, access, and distribution are. (Lecture 2, §2)
5. **B** — Standing is the honest answer to "why you, why now," grounded in experience, access, or distribution — not mere interest. (Lecture 2, §3)

</details>
