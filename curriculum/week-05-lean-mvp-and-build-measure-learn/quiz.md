# Week 5 — Quiz

Five questions. Notes closed. Answer key at the bottom.

---

**Q1.** An MVP is best understood as:

- A) Version one of the real product, just smaller.
- B) The smallest experiment that produces validated learning about the riskiest assumption.
- C) A fully featured beta.
- D) A pitch deck.

**Q2.** Your riskiest assumption is the one that is:

- A) Easiest and cheapest to test.
- B) Most fun to build.
- C) Both most uncertain and most fatal if false.
- D) The one investors care about.

**Q3.** A **Wizard of Oz** MVP:

- A) Is a fully automated product.
- B) Shows customers an automated experience while humans do the work behind the scenes.
- C) Is the same as a landing page.
- D) Requires the most engineering.

**Q4.** Why set the success threshold *before* launching?

- A) It is legally required.
- B) So you cannot rationalize a weak result into a "win."
- C) To impress the cohort.
- D) It makes the MVP cheaper to build.

**Q5.** A **pivot** is:

- A) Quitting the idea.
- B) Repeating the same experiment hoping for a better result.
- C) A change of direction grounded in what you learned.
- D) Raising money to keep going unchanged.

---

## Answer key

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

1. **B** — an experiment for validated learning. (L1 §1)
2. **C** — most uncertain and most fatal. (L1 §2)
3. **B** — humans behind the curtain. (L1 §3)
4. **B** — pre-commit to avoid moving goalposts. (L2 §2)
5. **C** — a learned change of direction. (L2 §4)

</details>
