# Week 5 — Exercises

Short, focused founder worksheets. These are **not coding exercises** — they're canvases and templates you fill in for *your own* idea. Do them in order; each one feeds the next, and all three feed the mini-project. Together they should take about 2.5–3 hours, spread across Monday–Tuesday so your MVP is fully designed *before* you build it.

The three exercises form a pipeline:

```
exercise-01  →  exercise-02  →  exercise-03
riskiest        MVP spec        experiment record
assumption      canvas          (fill AFTER launch)
(what to test)  (how to test)   (what happened)
```

## Index

1. **[Exercise 1 — Riskiest assumption](exercise-01-riskiest-assumption.md)** — surface every assumption your idea depends on, score them on uncertainty × fatality, and pick the one your MVP will test. Guided, with worked example. (~45 min)
2. **[Exercise 2 — MVP spec canvas](exercise-02-mvp-spec-canvas.md)** — a fill-in template that turns the chosen assumption into a runnable experiment: falsifiable hypothesis, MVP type, the one metric, the pre-committed threshold, the build plan, and the tools. (~50 min)
3. **[Exercise 3 — Experiment record template](exercise-03-experiment-record-template.md)** — the reusable record you fill in *after* you launch and measure: result vs. threshold, validated learning, and the named persevere/pivot decision. (~30 min to set up; you complete it after the run)

## How to work the exercises

- Work on **your own idea**, the one you carried out of Week 2 and validated in Week 3. Don't invent a fresh idea for this — the whole course compounds.
- **Be honest, not optimistic.** A worksheet you fudge to feel good wastes the week. Score the scary assumption high even though it's scary.
- **Test the scariest assumption, not the easiest.** If your MVP would take weeks of building, you almost certainly picked a build where a fake door or concierge would do.
- **Set the threshold before you launch, and never move it.** Exercise 2 makes you commit the number; Exercise 3 holds you to it.
- **Commit each worksheet to your Week 5 repo** as you finish it, so there's a timestamped paper trail (the threshold was set *before* the result — your git history proves it).

## Checklist

Mark each item when it's genuinely done — not when it's half-done and you've talked yourself into "good enough."

- [ ] Exercise 1: at least 8 assumptions listed and scored on both axes; one riskiest assumption chosen and stated as a hypothesis.
- [ ] Exercise 1: committed.
- [ ] Exercise 2: a falsifiable hypothesis in the "We believe… we'll know we're wrong if…" form.
- [ ] Exercise 2: an MVP type chosen, with a sentence justifying it as the *cheapest* test of *this* assumption.
- [ ] Exercise 2: exactly one metric, and a threshold committed *before* any build or launch.
- [ ] Exercise 2: a build plan you could finish in 2–3 days, with named free/low-cost tools.
- [ ] Exercise 2: committed (timestamp predates your launch).
- [ ] Exercise 3: template set up before launch; completed after the run with the real number, validated learning, and a named persevere/pivot decision.
- [ ] Exercise 3: committed.

There are no "solutions" to check in — these are about *your* startup. After the week, compare notes with your cohort: the most useful feedback is someone red-teaming your metric ("could you hit that and still have learned nothing?").
