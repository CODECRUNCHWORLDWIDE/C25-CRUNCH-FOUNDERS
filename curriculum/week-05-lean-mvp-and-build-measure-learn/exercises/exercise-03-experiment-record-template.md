## Worked example (Maya — concierge refill test), completed

- **Riskiest assumption:** front desks will hand off same-day refills.
- **MVP type:** concierge — Maya texted waitlisted patients by hand for 2 clinics; faked all "software." ~6 hours of her time, ~$8 in texting credits.
- **Hypothesis (locked):** front desks will let us run refills; right if refill rate ≥ 30% across 2 clinics in 1 week.
- **Result:** refill rate **45%** (9 of 20 cancellations), n=2 clinics, 1 week. **Pass.** Friends excluded; metric = the exact deed.
- **Validated learning:** moderate confidence that front desks adopt and manual refill works; surprise — patients responded fastest by text within 10 minutes, slowest by call.
- **Decision:** **PERSEVERE.** *"We refilled 45% against a 30% threshold, so we persevere to a willingness-to-pay test."*
- **Next experiment:** will clinics pay $99/mo? → Wizard-of-Oz product + Stripe payment link, threshold: 1 of 2 clinics commits to pay.
