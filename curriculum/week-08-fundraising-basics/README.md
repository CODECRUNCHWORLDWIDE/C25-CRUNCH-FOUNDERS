# Week 8 — Fundraising Basics

Raising money is a tool, not a trophy. This week demystifies early-stage fundraising so you can decide whether you even need it — and, if you do, understand what it costs in ownership and control. You will learn the difference between bootstrapping, angels, and pre-seed; how a cap table works; what SAFEs and convertibles do in plain terms; and how a round dilutes founders. This is also where your Week 1 control-vs-wealth lean becomes a concrete decision.

The deliverable is a cap-table model showing founder ownership before and after a hypothetical pre-seed round, with dilution computed.

## Learning objectives

By the end of this week, you will be able to:

- **Decide when *not* to raise** — and why raising too early can be a mistake.
- **Distinguish** bootstrapping, angels, and pre-seed/institutional money.
- **Read and build a basic cap table**.
- **Explain SAFEs and convertibles** in plain terms (valuation cap, discount, post-money), conceptually.
- **Compute dilution** across a priced round and an option pool.
- **Connect** the raise decision to your control-vs-wealth lean.

## Topics covered

- When not to raise: revenue, control, and the cost of capital
- Funding stages: bootstrapping → friends & family → angels → pre-seed → seed (overview)
- The cap table: shares, ownership %, and how new money changes it
- SAFEs and convertible notes in plain language; valuation cap and discount, conceptually
- Pre-money vs. post-money, and what "raising $X at $Y post" does to ownership
- The option pool and how it dilutes founders
- Dilution math across a round

## In-class activities

- **Raise or not?** Each founder argues whether their venture should raise now, given traction and their control-vs-wealth lean. The room scores the *reasoning*.
- **Cap-table build-along.** Everyone builds the same simple cap table in a sheet, then adds a round and watches the percentages move.
- **Dilution shock.** Add a 15% option pool *before* a round and watch founder ownership drop further than expected — the "pool shuffle" everyone meets eventually.

## Worked example

Two founders own 100% (say 8,000,000 shares total, 4M each). A pre-seed investor invests $500,000 at a $4.5M pre-money valuation. Post-money = $5.0M, so the investor gets $500k / $5.0M = **10%**. New shares are issued so the investor holds 10%; the founders' combined stake drops from 100% to ~90% (4.5M founder ownership weighting). If a 10% option pool is created pre-money, founders dilute *more*, because the pool comes out of their slice before the investor's money lands. The founder leaning toward *control* might raise less (or nothing) to keep ownership; the founder leaning toward *wealth* accepts the dilution to grow faster. The math is neutral; the choice is yours.
