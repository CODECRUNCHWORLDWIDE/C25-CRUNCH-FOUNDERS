# Week 8 — Resources

Every resource on this page is **free** and publicly accessible. You do not need an account to read the core material. We deliberately lean on primary sources — the people who write the standard documents, publish the standard term sheets, and disclose real market data — rather than secondhand blog summaries.

> **This is education, not investment, tax, or legal advice.** These links make you a *better-informed founder* and a *better client for a real lawyer*. They do not replace one. Anything you actually sign, you sign with counsel.

## Glossary cheat sheet

Keep this open in a tab while you build the model.

| Term | Plain English |
|------|---------------|
| **Bootstrapping** | Funding the company from savings or revenue. Maximum control, slower, no outside owners. |
| **Friends & family** | The earliest, relationship-laden money. Small checks. Paper it like a real investment or it poisons relationships. |
| **Angel** | An individual investing their own money, often early, often for the founder as much as the idea. |
| **Pre-seed** | The first institutional-ish money, usually on a SAFE, to reach a real product or early traction. |
| **Seed** | A larger round once there is traction; often the first priced (equity) round. |
| **Cap table** | The record of who owns what — shares and fully-diluted ownership %. |
| **Fully diluted** | Ownership counting *all* shares that could exist: issued shares plus the option pool plus convertibles. |
| **Pre-money valuation** | What the company is agreed to be worth *before* the new money goes in. |
| **Post-money valuation** | Pre-money + the amount invested. Investor % ≈ investment ÷ post-money. |
| **SAFE** | Simple Agreement for Future Equity. Money now, shares later, at a future priced round. |
| **Post-money SAFE** | The 2026-default YC SAFE: its cap is measured *after* the SAFE money, so your dilution from it is knowable on day one. |
| **Valuation cap** | The maximum valuation at which a SAFE converts. Protects the early investor if you later raise high. |
| **Discount** | A percent off the next round's price at which a SAFE converts (e.g., 20%). |
| **MFN** | "Most favored nation" — a SAFE clause letting an early investor adopt better terms you grant a later SAFE investor. |
| **Option pool** | Shares reserved for future employees. Usually created *pre-money*, so it dilutes founders. |
| **Pool shuffle** | The first-timer surprise: a pre-money pool comes out of *your* slice, not the investor's. |
| **Dilution** | Your ownership *percentage* falling as new shares are issued, even though your share count is unchanged. |
| **Pro-rata** | An investor's right to invest again in future rounds to maintain their percentage. |
| **Liquidation preference** | The order and multiple in which investors get paid back before common holders in a sale. (Mostly a priced-round term.) |
