# Week 8 — Exercises

Three focused worksheets. They are **not** code — they are the spreadsheets, templates, and read-throughs a founder actually produces. Do them in order; each one builds the muscle the next one needs, and together they assemble most of the mini-project.

> **This is education, not investment, tax, or legal advice.** The numbers and documents here are teaching instruments. Anything you'd actually sign, you sign with a lawyer.

## Index

1. **[Exercise 1 — Cap-Table Basics](exercise-01-cap-table-basics.md)** — build a starting cap table from a blank sheet and add one priced round. Guided, step by step, with the dilution math worked. (~45 min)
2. **[Exercise 2 — SAFE Conversion Worksheet](exercise-02-safe-conversion-worksheet.md)** — a fill-in template for stacking SAFEs and converting them at a priced round, so you see *before you sign* how much you've promised away. (~50 min)
3. **[Exercise 3 — Term-Sheet Read-Through](exercise-03-term-sheet-readthrough.md)** — an annotation template for marking up a real pre-seed term sheet: what matters, what's boilerplate, what you'd push back on. (~45 min)

## How to work the exercises

- **Use a real spreadsheet** (Google Sheets or LibreOffice Calc) for Exercises 1 and 2. Type the formulas yourself — don't hard-code the percentages. The whole point is that when you change one input, the ownership column recomputes and you *see* the dilution move.
- **Sanity-check every table.** Ownership percentages must sum to **100.0%**. If they don't, you've issued or dropped shares somewhere. A table that doesn't sum to 100 is wrong, full stop.
- **Show your work.** For every percentage, you should be able to point at the division that produced it: `shares ÷ total`, or `investment ÷ post-money`. If you can't, you copied a number instead of computing it.
- **Tie it to your own venture.** Use *your* founder split from Week 4 and a raise scenario that fits *your* Week 6 economics. Generic numbers teach the mechanic; your numbers teach the decision.
- **Commit your work** to your Week 8 repo — export the sheet to CSV or screenshot it, and commit the markdown worksheets.

## Checklist

Mark each done when the acceptance criteria in that file are met:

- [ ] **Exercise 1** — starting table sums to 100%; one priced round added; pre/post-money and investor % stated; founder before/after dilution shown.
- [ ] **Exercise 2** — at least two SAFEs stacked; each SAFE's implied % computed against its cap; total pre-priced-round dilution summed; one conversion at a priced round worked.
- [ ] **Exercise 3** — a sample term sheet annotated; the 4–5 terms that matter flagged; at least one term you'd negotiate, with your reasoning; boilerplate identified as such.

There are no answer keys checked in for the spreadsheet exercises — the numbers depend on *your* inputs. Exercise 1 walks the math explicitly so you can verify your own. After you finish, compare with a cohort-mate: same scenario, same numbers, or someone made an error worth finding.

When all three feel comfortable, you have most of the [mini-project](../mini-project/README.md) already in hand.
