# Mini-Project — Cap-Table + Dilution Model

> Build a spreadsheet that shows founder ownership before and after a hypothetical pre-seed round, including an option pool, with dilution computed — and a short memo on whether you would actually raise. The dilution insight feeds the "ask" slide of your capstone pitch.

**This is education, not investment or legal advice.**

**Estimated time:** ~1.75 hours.

## What you will produce

1. **A cap-table spreadsheet** (Google Sheets / LibreOffice; export CSV or screenshot into `cap-table/`) modeling:
   - Starting ownership (founders, any early holders), summing to 100%.
   - An option pool (e.g., 10%) created pre-money.
   - A pre-seed investment with stated pre-money and post-money.
   - Post-round ownership for every holder, summing to 100%.
   - Founder ownership before vs. after, and the % surrendered.
2. **`cap-table/RAISE-MEMO.md`** — half a page: would you raise this round? Why or why not, tied explicitly to your control-vs-wealth lean and your unit economics from Week 6.

---

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] Spreadsheet with starting and post-round tables, each summing to 100%.
- [ ] Option pool modeled pre-money.
- [ ] Pre-money and post-money stated; investor % consistent with them.
- [ ] Founder dilution shown before/after.
- [ ] A raise memo tied to the control/wealth lean.
- [ ] A visible "not investment/legal advice" note.
- [ ] Committed (sheet exported/screenshotted).

## Rubric

| Criterion | Weight |
|-----------|------:|
| Cap-table math is correct (sums to 100%, investor % matches post-money) | 35% |
| Option pool dilution modeled correctly | 25% |
| Founder before/after dilution shown clearly | 20% |
| Raise memo reasons from lean + economics | 20% |
