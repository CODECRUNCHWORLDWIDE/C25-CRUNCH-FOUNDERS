# Week 8 — Challenges

The exercises drill the mechanics — cap tables, SAFE math, term-sheet reading. **Challenges stretch you into judgment.** Where an exercise has one right number, a challenge is a realistic founder *decision* with several defensible answers and one job for you: reason it through, in numbers and in values, and defend it.

## Index

1. **[Challenge 1 — The Down-Round Decision](challenge-01-the-down-round-decision.md)** — your runway is running out, the market has turned, and you must choose between a dilutive bridge, a painful down round, a hard cut to default-alive, or walking away. Model each path's cap table and make the call. (~90–120 min)

## What a challenge is

A challenge is a **decision scenario**, not a worksheet. You're handed a situation a real founder faces, with messy, conflicting pressures, and asked to:

- **Model the options** — build the cap table or runway math for each realistic path, so the choice rests on numbers, not vibes.
- **Make a call** — pick one path and commit to it, explicitly.
- **Defend it** — tie the decision to your control-vs-wealth lean (Week 1), your unit economics (Week 6), and the cost of capital (this week). A good defense survives the obvious objection.

There is no single correct answer. A control-leaning founder and a wealth-leaning founder can reach opposite conclusions from the *same* numbers and both be right — *if* the reasoning holds.

## How it's assessed

Challenges are **optional but recommended.** You can pass the week without them; doing them puts you measurably ahead, and the down-round muscle here is exactly what your capstone "ask" slide and its hard-question defense will lean on in Week 10.

| Criterion | What "great" looks like |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| **Modeling** | Each realistic path has a cap table or runway model with the math shown; the numbers are internally consistent and sum correctly. |
| **Decision** | One path is chosen explicitly and unambiguously — no "it depends" hedge that avoids deciding. |
| **Reasoning** | The choice is defended from the founder's lean and economics; the trade-offs of the *rejected* paths are acknowledged, not ignored. |
| **Hard question** | The write-up anticipates and answers the single most obvious objection to the chosen path. |
| **Honesty** | No vanity, no hand-waving. If the chosen path is genuinely bad and just least-bad, it says so. |

Submit your challenge work (model + write-up) to your Week 8 repo under `challenges/`. This is education, not investment or legal advice.
