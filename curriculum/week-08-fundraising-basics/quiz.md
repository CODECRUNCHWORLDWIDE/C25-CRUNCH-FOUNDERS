# Week 8 — Quiz

Six questions. Notes closed. Answer key at the bottom.

---

**Q1.** The best time to raise is generally:

- A) As early as possible, before you have customers.
- B) When you do not desperately need to — when traction gives you leverage.
- C) Only after you are profitable.
- D) Never; raising is always a mistake.

**Q2.** Bootstrapping means:

- A) Raising a large pre-seed round.
- B) Funding the company from savings or revenue, keeping maximum control.
- C) Taking angel money only.
- D) Issuing a SAFE.

**Q3.** Investor ownership in a priced round is approximately:

- A) Investment ÷ pre-money.
- B) Investment ÷ post-money.
- C) Pre-money ÷ post-money.
- D) Investment × discount.

**Q4.** A SAFE is:

- A) A bank loan.
- B) An agreement where an investor gives money now for shares later, at a future priced round.
- C) A grant that never dilutes.
- D) A type of option pool.

**Q5.** If a 10% option pool is created **pre-money**:

- A) Investors absorb the dilution.
- B) Founders dilute more, because the pool comes out of their slice before the money lands.
- C) Nobody dilutes.
- D) The post-money valuation drops.

**Q6.** Dilution refers to:

- A) Losing share count.
- B) Your ownership *percentage* falling as new shares are issued, even if your share count is unchanged.
- C) The company losing value.
- D) Selling your shares.

---

## Answer key

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

1. **B** — raise from leverage, not desperation. (L1 §1)
2. **B** — self/revenue funded, max control. (L1 §3)
3. **B** — investment ÷ post-money. (L2 §2)
4. **B** — money now, shares later. (L2 §3)
5. **B** — pre-money pool dilutes founders more. (L2 §4)
6. **B** — percentage falls as shares are issued. (L2 §1)

</details>
