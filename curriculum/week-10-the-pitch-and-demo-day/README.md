# Week 10 — The Pitch + Demo Day (Capstone)

Everything you have built this course — the discovery dossier, the MVP result, the lean canvas and unit economics, the go-to-market test, the cap-table model, the co-founder terms — becomes a three-minute story. The pitch is not where you start believing your idea; it is where you *present the evidence* you gathered. A good early-stage pitch is evidence wearing a clear structure, not adjectives wearing a logo. This week you assemble the deck, record the pitch, rehearse the hard questions, and present at demo day.

The capstone is a validated startup concept: ≥10 synthesized discovery interviews, a lean business model canvas, an MVP or clickable prototype, a pitch deck, and a 3-minute recorded pitch.

## Learning objectives

By the end of this week, you will be able to:

- **Structure a 3-minute pitch**: problem → evidence → solution → model → traction → ask.
- **Build a lean deck** (about 8–10 slides) where each slide carries one idea.
- **Lead with evidence**, not with vision — your interviews and MVP result are the credibility.
- **Record and edit** a clean 3-minute pitch.
- **Anticipate and answer** the three most obvious hard questions.
- **Assemble the full capstone** repo and present at demo day.

## Topics covered

- The 3-minute pitch structure and timing
- The lean deck: one idea per slide, evidence over adjectives
- Telling the discovery story honestly (including what you learned/pivoted)
- The "ask": what you actually want from the audience
- Recording cleanly (free tools) and editing to time
- Handling hard questions without defensiveness

## In-class activities

- **Pitch gauntlet.** Everyone gives their 3-minute pitch to the room and gets timed; over-time pitches are cut off, exactly as at a real demo day.
- **Hard-question fire drill.** The room throws the three obvious tough questions at each founder; calm, evidence-based answers score.
- **Deck red-pen.** Pairs swap decks and delete every adjective that is not backed by evidence.

## Worked example

The clinic founder's 3-minute pitch: **Problem** (no-shows cost small clinics real revenue — 20s). **Evidence** (12 interviews; clinics already pay for two clumsy tools; cited cost per no-show — 40s). **Solution** (waitlist auto-fill — the pivot her discovery surfaced — 30s, with a 10s clickable-prototype clip). **Model** ($99/month, ~7:1 LTV:CAC, ~3-month payback — 30s). **Traction** (2 paying clinics from founder-led sales, leak identified at demo→trial — 30s). **Ask** (what she wants now — 20s). Every claim traces to a deliverable in her repo. No "revolutionary," no "huge market" hand-waving — just evidence, clearly told.
