# Week 10 — Quiz

Five questions. Notes closed. Answer key at the bottom.

---

**Q1.** A strong early-stage pitch is best described as:

- A) A vision statement full of ambitious adjectives.
- B) Evidence wearing a clear structure.
- C) A live coding demo.
- D) A detailed financial forecast.

**Q2.** The recommended 3-minute structure opens with:

- A) The ask.
- B) The team's resumes.
- C) The problem and the evidence it is real.
- D) The valuation.

**Q3.** If your discovery led to a pivot, in the pitch you should:

- A) Hide it for a cleaner story.
- B) Say so — it signals disciplined, evidence-led founding.
- C) Pretend the original idea is unchanged.
- D) Skip the evidence slide.

**Q4.** The best response to a hard question you cannot fully answer is:

- A) Argue with the question.
- B) Bluff confidently.
- C) "We don't know yet, and here's how we'd find out."
- D) Change the subject.

**Q5.** Which counts as the strongest traction on a pitch?

- A) Newsletter signups.
- B) Social followers.
- C) Paying customers.
- D) Website visits.

---

## Answer key

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

1. **B** — evidence in a clear structure. (L1 §1)
2. **C** — problem then evidence. (L1 §2)
3. **B** — own the pivot; it is a strength. (L1 §4)
4. **C** — honesty plus a test plan beats bluffing. (L2 §4)
5. **C** — paying customers over vanity metrics. (L1 §1; Week 7)

</details>
