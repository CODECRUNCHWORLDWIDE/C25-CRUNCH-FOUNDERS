# Week 10 — Resources

Curated, current to 2026, and free unless marked. Each link has a one-line note on *why* it's here and *when* to reach for it. If a link 404s, open an issue.

## Start here (the two things to read before Monday's lecture)

- **Y Combinator — "How to Pitch Your Startup" / "How to Design a Better Pitch Deck"** (YC Library, free): <https://www.ycombinator.com/library> — the canonical short-deck structure and the "one idea per slide" discipline; YC has funded thousands of seed companies and this is their house style.
- **Sequoia Capital — "Writing a Business Plan" (the pitch deck template)**: <https://www.sequoiacap.com/article/writing-a-business-plan/> — the ten-slide skeleton most seed decks descend from; read it for the *order* of the story, not the formatting.

## Pitch structure and storytelling

- **Kawasaki — the 10/20/30 rule** (10 slides, 20 minutes, 30-point font): search "Guy Kawasaki 10 20 30" — a useful counterweight that argues *fewer* slides, *bigger* type; adapt the spirit (cut, simplify) to the 3-minute format.
- **Andreessen Horowitz — "16 Startup Metrics" and "The Only 2 Metrics That Matter"**: <https://a16z.com/16-startup-metrics/> — so the *traction* slide cites the metric investors actually weigh, not a vanity number.
- **First Round Review — pitch and storytelling essays**: <https://review.firstround.com/> — operator-grade writing on how founders actually frame the problem and the ask; search "pitch" and "fundraising."
- **Wikipedia — Pitch deck** and **Elevator pitch**: <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pitch_deck> · <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elevator_pitch> — neutral, plain-English definitions if a term is unfamiliar.

## Real demo-day and seed decks (study the structure, don't copy the words)

- **DocSend / Pitch — public deck galleries**: search "successful seed pitch deck examples 2026" and read 3–4; notice that the strong ones lead with the problem and the evidence, and the weak ones lead with the product.
- **Airbnb, Front, and Uber original decks** (widely republished): study how few words per slide and how concrete the early-stage claims are.
- **Y Combinator Demo Day format notes**: <https://www.ycombinator.com/library> — what a 60-second-to-2-minute demo-day pitch actually contains, since this is the format your 3-minute version compresses from.

## Fundraising literacy (for the "ask" and the hard questions)

- **Y Combinator — the SAFE and "A Guide to Seed Fundraising"**: <https://www.ycombinator.com/documents> — the post-money SAFE primer and templates; reach for this when you write the raise-memo version of your ask (stretch goal) or get the "how much are you raising and on what terms" question.
- **Carta — cap table and SAFE explainers**: <https://carta.com/learn/> — plain-language pieces on dilution, valuation caps, and discounts; pairs with your Week 08 cap-table model.
- **Paul Graham — essays on startups and fundraising**: <https://paulgraham.com/articles.html> — "How to Raise Money," "Default Alive or Default Dead," and "Do Things That Don't Scale" inform the traction and ask slides directly.

## Free recording and editing tools

- **OBS Studio** (free, open-source, screen + camera): <https://obsproject.com/> — the workhorse for recording your deck with voice; splice in the prototype clip.
- **Loom** (free tier): <https://www.loom.com/> — fastest path to a shareable recording link; fine for the 3-minute pitch if you don't need heavy editing.
- **Shotcut** (free, open-source video editor): <https://shotcut.org/> — cut to time and trim filler without paying for an editor.
- **Audacity** (free, open-source audio): <https://www.audacityteam.org/> — clean up audio (noise reduction, leveling); remember audio quality matters more than video.

## Free slide and deck tools

- **Google Slides**: <https://slides.google.com/> — collaborative, exports clean PDF; the default for most.
- **Marp** (Markdown → slides): <https://marp.app/> — version-control your deck as text; ideal if you want the deck to live in the same repo as your evidence.
- **reveal.js** (open-source HTML slides): <https://revealjs.com/> — if you want the deck to be a web page you can link.
- **Pitch** (free tier): <https://pitch.com/> — purpose-built for startup decks with strong templates.

## Glossary

| Term | Plain English |
|------|---------------|
| **Pitch deck** | The ~8–10 slide story of the company, one idea per slide |
| **The ask** | The single specific thing you want from this audience |
| **Demo day** | The event where founders present pitches and take questions |
| **Evidence-first** | Leading with proof (interviews, traction), not adjectives |
| **Riskiest assumption** | The thing most likely to sink the company; name it calmly |
| **Traction** | Real evidence of demand — paying customers beat signups beat followers |
| **SAFE** | Simple Agreement for Future Equity — the standard early-stage investment instrument; converts to shares later |
| **Post-money valuation cap** | The ceiling valuation at which a SAFE converts; lower cap = more ownership for the investor |
| **Founder-led sales** | The founder personally closing the first customers; the most common early GTM motion |
| **LTV:CAC** | Lifetime value over customer-acquisition cost; the ratio that says whether scaling helps or hurts |
