# Week 2 — Resources

All free and publicly accessible.

## Required

- **Y Combinator Startup School — "How to Get Startup Ideas"** (free talk/essay material): <https://www.startupschool.org/library>
- **Paul Graham — "How to Get Startup Ideas"**: <https://www.paulgraham.com/startupideas.html>
- **Paul Graham — "Do Things That Don't Scale"** (why early validation is manual): <https://www.paulgraham.com/ds.html>

## Free desk-validation tools

- **Google Trends** — relative search demand over time: <https://trends.google.com/trends/>
- **Reddit** — search a subreddit for people describing the problem in their own words: <https://www.reddit.com/>
- **Wikipedia — Market research** — neutral overview of validation approaches: <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Market_research>

## On problems vs. solutions

- **Steve Blank — Customer Development** (free blog posts on starting from the problem): <https://steveblank.com/category/customer-development/>
- **Wikipedia — Problem statement**: <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Problem_statement>

## Glossary

| Term | Plain English |
|------|---------------|
| **Problem-first** | Start from a real problem; decide the solution last |
| **Triage** | Scoring many candidate ideas to keep one |
| **Desk validation** | Cheap research before talking to customers |
| **Willingness-to-pay signal** | Evidence people already spend to fix the problem |
| **Kill criteria** | Pre-committed evidence that would make you drop an idea |
