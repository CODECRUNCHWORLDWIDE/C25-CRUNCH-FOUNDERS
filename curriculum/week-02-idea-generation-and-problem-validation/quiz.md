# Week 2 — Quiz

Five multiple-choice questions. Notes closed. Answer key at the bottom.

---

**Q1.** "Problem-first" idea generation means:

- A) Picking the most technically impressive solution and finding a use for it.
- B) Starting from a felt, recurring problem and deciding the solution last.
- C) Copying whatever competitor is growing fastest.
- D) Protecting your idea as a secret until launch.

**Q2.** Which is the *strongest* willingness-to-pay signal during desk validation?

- A) The problem sounds interesting on a podcast.
- B) People already pay for a clumsy existing tool that addresses it.
- C) You personally think it should exist.
- D) A competitor recently shut down.

**Q3.** Why write **kill criteria** before talking to customers?

- A) To impress investors.
- B) To pre-commit to evidence that would make you drop the idea, fighting confirmation bias.
- C) Because every idea must be killed eventually.
- D) To make the idea harder to copy.

**Q4.** A candidate scores high on painkiller-ness and pay signal but you have no way to reach the customers. The honest read is:

- A) Build immediately; the pain is real.
- B) Reachability is a real constraint — without it you cannot do discovery, so it is weaker than it looks for *you*.
- C) Ignore reachability; it sorts itself out later.
- D) Raise money first to buy access.

**Q5.** You leave Week 2 carrying how many ideas into discovery?

- A) As many as scored above 15.
- B) Three, to hedge.
- C) One — the highest-scoring, reachable, kill-criteria-armed idea.
- D) Zero; discovery generates the idea.

---

## Answer key

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

1. **B** — problem first, solution last. (L1 §1)
2. **B** — existing paid workarounds prove pain. (L2 §2)
3. **B** — pre-committed kill criteria defeat confirmation bias. (L2 §3)
4. **B** — reachability gates your ability to validate at all. (L2 §1)
5. **C** — one idea, done justice. (L2 §4)

</details>
