# Lecture 2 — Triage and Desk Validation

> **Duration:** ~45 min. **Outcome:** You can score candidate ideas against a problem-first filter, run free desk validation, and write kill criteria before you commit.

## 1. The triage filter

Score each candidate 1–5 on each axis. Be honest; inflating scores only fools you.

| Axis | Question | 1 | 5 |
|------|----------|---|---|
| Painkiller-ness | Is the pain urgent and costly? | Mild "nice to have" | People hack workarounds today |
| Frequency | How often does it bite? | Once a year | Daily / weekly |
| Standing | Why you, why now? | No access | Lived it; warm contacts |
| Reachability | Can you find these people cheaply? | No idea where they are | A list you can message this week |
| Pay signal | Is anyone already paying to fix it? | No evidence | Existing paid workarounds |

A candidate that scores 20+ and is reachable is worth interviewing. One in the low teens is usually a vitamin or a space you lack standing in.

## 2. Desk validation (free, before interviews)

You can learn a lot in an hour without talking to anyone:

- **Search demand.** Are people searching for this problem or workarounds? Free tools: Google Trends, autocomplete suggestions, Reddit and forum threads.
- **Existing alternatives.** What do people use today — a competitor, a spreadsheet, duct tape, or nothing? "Nothing" can mean no pain *or* an unserved market; interviews tell you which.
- **What they pay for.** Existing paid tools, even bad ones, are a strong willingness-to-pay signal.

Desk validation never *confirms* an idea — only interviews do that — but it cheaply kills obvious losers.

## 3. Kill criteria: decide what would end it, now

Before you get attached, write down the evidence that would make you drop the idea. Examples:

- "If fewer than half of ten interviewees describe this as a real, recurring pain, I drop it."
- "If everyone is happy with their current workaround, I drop it."
- "If I cannot find ten reachable people in two weeks, I drop it."

Pre-committed kill criteria are the antidote to confirmation bias. Without them you will hear "yes" in every "maybe."

## 4. Choose one

Pick the single highest-scoring, reachable, kill-criteria-armed idea to carry into Week 3. One. Carrying three ideas into discovery means doing none of them justice.
