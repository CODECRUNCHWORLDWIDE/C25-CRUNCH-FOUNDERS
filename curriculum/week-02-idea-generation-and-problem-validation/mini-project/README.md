# Mini-Project — The Idea-Triage Log

> Score 5–10 candidate ideas against the problem-first filter, run desk validation on the top two, and commit to one idea — with kill criteria — to carry into Week 3 discovery.

**Estimated time:** ~1.75 hours.

## What you will produce

A file `IDEA-TRIAGE-LOG.md` in your Week 2 repo containing a triage table, desk-validation notes for the top two candidates, and a final commitment.

### 1. Triage table

| Idea (problem, no solution) | Painkiller | Frequency | Standing | Reachability | Pay signal | Total /25 |
|------------------------------|:---------:|:---------:|:--------:|:------------:|:----------:|:---------:|
| ... | 4 | 5 | 5 | 4 | 4 | 22 |

Score each axis 1–5 honestly. At least five rows.

### 2. Desk validation (top two)

For each of the two highest scorers: search demand, named existing alternatives, willingness-to-pay signal, and a one-line verdict.

### 3. The commitment

- **The one idea I carry into Week 3:** (state the problem, not the solution.)
- **Why this one** (two sentences referencing the scores and validation).
- **Kill criteria** (3–5 specific, falsifiable conditions).
- **My first five interview targets** (by role; no private info).

---

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] File at `mini-project/IDEA-TRIAGE-LOG.md`.
- [ ] Triage table with at least five scored ideas.
- [ ] Desk validation for the top two candidates.
- [ ] Exactly one idea committed, stated as a problem.
- [ ] 3–5 falsifiable kill criteria.
- [ ] Five interview targets named by role.
- [ ] Committed.

## Rubric

| Criterion | Weight |
|-----------|------:|
| Ideas stated as problems, not solutions | 25% |
| Honest scoring (not all 5s) | 20% |
| Desk validation cites real alternatives | 20% |
| Kill criteria are specific and falsifiable | 20% |
| A single, reachable idea is committed | 15% |
