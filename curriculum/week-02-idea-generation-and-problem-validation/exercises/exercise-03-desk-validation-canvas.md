# Exercise 3 — Desk-Validation Canvas

**Type:** Fillable canvas. Copy this whole file into your Week 2 repo as `exercises/exercise-03-desk-validation-canvas.md`, then fill one full canvas for **each of your top two candidates** from Exercise 2. Commit when done.

**Goal:** Run the four desk-validation passes — demand, existing alternatives, pay signal, incumbent reviews — on your top two candidates, using only free tools, in under an hour each. End each canvas with a one-line verdict: stronger or weaker than you thought.

**Estimated time:** ~60 minutes per idea (so ~2 hours for both).

---

## Before you start

- This is **research, not building.** No landing page, no Figma, no survey. Just a browser and the free tools below.
- **Name things specifically, with links.** "There are some competitors" is worthless. "Curogram and NexHealth both send reminders; neither auto-fills a canceled slot from a waitlist" is validation.
- **Weight costly signals over free ones.** A paid tool with angry reviews beats a hundred "this sounds cool" comments (Lecture 2, §3).
- Free tools: Google Trends, Google search autocomplete + "People also ask", Reddit (`site:reddit.com <problem>`), Answer the Public, app-store reviews (App Store / Google Play), G2 / Capterra / Trustpilot, competitor pricing pages, Crunchbase free tier.

Duplicate the canvas below for each idea. Two canvases total.
