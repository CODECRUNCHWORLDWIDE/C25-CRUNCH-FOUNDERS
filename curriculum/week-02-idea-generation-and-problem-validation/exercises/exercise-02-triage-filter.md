# Exercise 2 — Triage Filter Worksheet

**Type:** Fillable worksheet / scoring canvas. Copy this whole file into your Week 2 repo as `exercises/exercise-02-triage-filter.md`, replace the example rows with your own shortlist from Exercise 1, and commit.

**Goal:** Score each shortlisted problem on the five-axis triage filter, honestly, and rank them. The top two move into Exercise 3 (desk validation).

**Estimated time:** 45 minutes.

---

## How to use this worksheet

1. Pull your 5–10 shortlisted problems from [Exercise 1](exercise-01-problem-inventory.md).
2. Score each on all five axes, 1–5, using the rubric below. **Do not look at the total while scoring an axis** — score each axis on its own merits, then sum.
3. After scoring, find your top idea's **soft spot** — the axis where it's weakest — and write one honest sentence about it. If your top idea has no axis below 5, you are inflating. Re-score.
4. Sort by total. The top two go to Exercise 3.

> **The honesty rule:** a column of all-5s is a bug, not a win. Real painkiller problems have a soft spot somewhere. If yours don't, you're scoring the idea you wish you had.

---

## The scoring rubric (keep this open while you score)

| Score | Painkiller-ness | Frequency | Standing | Reachability | Pay signal |
|------:|-----------------|-----------|----------|--------------|------------|
| **5** | Workarounds used today, under pressure | Daily | Lived it + warm list in hand | Can message 10+ this week | Paid tools exist; people switch for better |
| **4** | Clear cost, actively acted on | Weekly | Worked adjacent + some contacts | Know exactly where they gather | Paid tools exist; sticky |
| **3** | Real but tolerated | Monthly | Understand the domain | Findable with effort | People spend *hours* (not $) on it |
| **2** | Mild annoyance | Quarterly | Outsider with interest | Vague idea where they are | Occasional ad-hoc spend |
| **1** | Nice-to-have | Yearly or less | No access | No idea where they are | No evidence anyone acts |

**What each axis protects against:** painkiller (vitamins don't sell under pressure) · frequency (no habit / no recurring revenue) · standing (someone should build it, but not you) · reachability (the silent killer — you can't even run discovery) · pay signal (market hasn't priced the pain).

## Common scoring mistakes (read before you score)

- **Scoring the solution, not the problem.** The filter is about the *problem*. "My solution would be really good" is not painkiller-ness.
- **Rounding reachability up.** "I could probably find them" is a 2, not a 4. A 4 means you know exactly where they gather. A 5 means you can message ten this week.
- **Confusing 'I'd use it' with a pay signal.** Your own enthusiasm is not market spend. Pay signal is about *other people's* money or hours.
- **Letting the total drive the axis scores.** Score each axis blind to the total, then add. If you peek, you'll nudge axes to hit a number you like.
