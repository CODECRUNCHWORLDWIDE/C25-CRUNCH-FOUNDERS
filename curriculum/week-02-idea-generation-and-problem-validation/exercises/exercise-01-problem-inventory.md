# Exercise 1 — Problem Inventory

**Goal:** Generate a problem-first inventory and narrow it to the candidates worth triaging in the mini-project.

**Estimated time:** 60 minutes.

---

## What to produce

Create `exercises/exercise-01-problem-inventory.md`:

1. **Raw list.** At least **15 problems** you have felt or observed, each stated in one sentence *without* a solution. Pull from all four sources in Lecture 1 §2 (your friction, your industry, expert annoyances, expensive workarounds).
2. **First cut.** Mark each as painkiller (P) or vitamin (V) with a one-word reason.
3. **Shortlist.** Pull the 5–10 painkillers where you also have standing. These feed the [mini-project](../mini-project/README.md) triage log.

```markdown
# Problem inventory — <your name>

## Raw list (15+)
1. (problem, no solution) — P/V — reason
...

## Shortlist (5–10 with standing)
- ...
```

---

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] File at `exercises/exercise-01-problem-inventory.md`.
- [ ] At least 15 problems, each stated without a solution.
- [ ] Each labeled P or V with a reason.
- [ ] A shortlist of 5–10 painkillers where you have standing.
- [ ] Committed.

---

## Hints

- If you keep writing solutions instead of problems, add "the problem is that…" in front of each line and rewrite.
- Most of your 15 will be vitamins. Good. That is the point of generating quantity.
