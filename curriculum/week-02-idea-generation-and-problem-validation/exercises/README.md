# Week 2 — Exercises

Three focused exercises that take you from a blank page to a scored, desk-validated shortlist. Do them in order — each one feeds the next, and all three feed the [mini-project](../mini-project/README.md) (the idea-triage log).

These are **founder worksheets**, not coding drills. Exercise 1 is a guided generation exercise. Exercises 2 and 3 are fillable markdown templates — copy them into your Week 2 repo, fill in your own content, and commit. There is no code anywhere this week.

## Index

1. **[Exercise 1 — Problem inventory](exercise-01-problem-inventory.md)** — generate 15+ problems problem-first from the four wells, then first-cut to a shortlist of painkillers where you have standing. (~60 min)
2. **[Exercise 2 — Triage filter worksheet](exercise-02-triage-filter.md)** — score your shortlist on the five-axis filter, honestly, in a fillable table. (~45 min)
3. **[Exercise 3 — Desk-validation canvas](exercise-03-desk-validation-canvas.md)** — run the four desk-validation passes on your top two candidates using free tools. (~60 min per idea)

## How to work the exercises

- **Generate quantity before judging quality.** Exercise 1's only goal is volume. Don't filter while you generate.
- **State every problem without naming a solution.** If you catch yourself writing "an app that…", cross it out and write the pain instead. Use the one-sentence problem test (Lecture 1, §2) on every line.
- **Score honestly in Exercise 2.** A column of all-5s is a bug. Find each idea's real soft spot and score it (Lecture 2, §1).
- **Use only free tools in Exercise 3.** Google Trends, search autocomplete, Reddit, app-store and G2 reviews, competitor pricing pages. No paid keyword tools, no surveys.
- **Weight costly signals over free ones.** A paid competitor with bad reviews beats a hundred "this sounds cool" comments.
- **Commit as you go.** Each exercise produces a markdown file in your Week 2 repo. You'll reference them in the mini-project and again in Weeks 3 and 6.

## Checklist

Work through these in order. Tick each when its artifact is committed.

- [ ] **Ex 1** — `exercises/exercise-01-problem-inventory.md` with 15+ problems, each P/V-labeled, and a 5–10 item shortlist of painkillers with standing.
- [ ] **Ex 2** — `exercises/exercise-02-triage-filter.md` filled in: every shortlist idea scored 1–5 on all five axes, summed /25, sorted, with the soft spot of your top idea named.
- [ ] **Ex 3** — `exercises/exercise-03-desk-validation-canvas.md` completed for your **top two** candidates: demand, named alternatives (with links), pay signal, review findings, and a one-line verdict each.
- [ ] All three files committed to your Week 2 repo with sensible messages.
- [ ] You can name, out loud, your single highest-scoring reachable idea and why it beat the runner-up.

When all three are done, you have everything the [mini-project](../mini-project/README.md) needs — the triage log is mostly assembly at that point.
