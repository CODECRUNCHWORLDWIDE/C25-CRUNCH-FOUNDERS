# Week 2 Homework

Three problems, about **2 hours** total. Work in your Week 2 repo.

---

## Problem 1 — Reverse three solutions to their problems

**Problem statement.** Take three "cool product ideas" (yours or ones you have heard). At `notes/reverse-solutions.md`, trace each back to the problem it supposedly solves. For each, state whether a real painkiller problem survives the reversal.

**Acceptance criteria.** File present; three reversals; each labeled painkiller/vitamin/none. Committed.

**Hint.** At least one will reveal "no actual problem." That is the lesson.

**Estimated time.** 30 min.

---

## Problem 2 — Desk validation on your chosen idea

**Problem statement.** For your top candidate, run desk validation at `notes/desk-validation.md`: search demand (what you found), existing alternatives (named), and any willingness-to-pay signal. End with a one-line verdict: stronger or weaker than you thought.

**Acceptance criteria.** File present; all three sections; named alternatives with links; a verdict. Committed.

**Hint.** Use free tools only — Google Trends, search autocomplete, Reddit/forums, competitor pricing pages.

**Estimated time.** 45 min.

---

## Problem 3 — Write your kill criteria

**Problem statement.** At `notes/kill-criteria.md`, write three to five specific, falsifiable kill criteria for your chosen idea — the evidence from Week 3 interviews that would make you drop it.

**Acceptance criteria.** File present; 3–5 criteria; each is specific and falsifiable (a number or clear threshold). Committed.

**Hint.** "If fewer than 5 of 10 interviewees call this a recurring pain, I drop it" is good. "If it seems bad" is not.

**Estimated time.** 30 min.
