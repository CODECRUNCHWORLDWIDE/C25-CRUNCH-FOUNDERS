# Week 2 — Challenges

The exercises drill the core motion: generate, score, validate. **Challenges stretch you** with a realistic founder decision under pressure, where the answer isn't on a rubric and reasonable people could disagree. Each one takes 60–120 minutes and produces a written decision you can defend — the kind of artifact that shows up in your portfolio and, later, in how you reason on stage at demo day.

## Index

1. **[Challenge 1 — Vitamin or painkiller? The three-idea triage call](challenge-01-vitamin-or-painkiller.md)** — you're handed three real-feeling startup ideas with messy, partial evidence. Score them, kill two, defend the survivor, and write its kill criteria. (~90 min)

## How challenges are assessed

Challenges are **optional** — you can pass the week without them. But the founder who does them is measurably sharper than the one who didn't, and this one rehearses the exact judgment the capstone (Week 10) tests: can you look at incomplete evidence, make a defensible call, and survive the obvious hard question?

A strong challenge submission shows:

- **A decision, not a hedge.** You picked one. You killed the others explicitly.
- **Reasoning tied to evidence.** Every score and kill is justified by something in the scenario, not vibes.
- **Honesty about uncertainty.** You name what you *don't* know and how you'd find out — instead of pretending the evidence is cleaner than it is.
- **Falsifiable kill criteria.** Numbers and thresholds, not "if it seems bad."
- **It survives the hard question.** The challenge ends with a devil's-advocate prompt; a strong answer doesn't crumble under it.

There is no single correct answer. Two founders can reach opposite calls and both score well *if the reasoning is sound and the evidence is read honestly.* What fails is hedging, hand-waving, vanity signals treated as validation, and kill criteria you couldn't actually test.

Commit your written decision to your Week 2 repo under `challenges/challenge-01/`. This judgment muscle reappears in Week 3 (deciding when interviews have killed an idea), Week 5 (persevere-or-pivot), and Week 10 (defending the pitch).
