# Week 3 — Quiz

Five questions. Notes closed. Answer key at the bottom.

---

**Q1.** The primary purpose of a discovery interview is to:

- A) Sell the customer on your solution.
- B) Learn the truth about a problem without pitching.
- C) Collect email signups.
- D) Get a quote for your landing page.

**Q2.** Which question yields the most reliable data?

- A) "Would you use an app that does this?"
- B) "Don't you hate when this happens?"
- C) "Tell me about the last time this happened and what it cost you."
- D) "Do you like my idea?"

**Q3.** The **compliment trap** is:

- A) Paying interviewees too much.
- B) Friendly people saying your idea is great, which feels like validation but is worthless data.
- C) Complimenting the interviewee to build rapport.
- D) Only interviewing friends.

**Q4.** Roughly how much should the interviewer talk?

- A) About 80% — you must explain the vision.
- B) About 20% — listen far more than you speak.
- C) Exactly half.
- D) As little as possible; stay silent the whole time.

**Q5.** Your ten interviews trip a Week 2 kill criterion. The right response is:

- A) Ignore it; you're committed now.
- B) Re-run interviews until you get the answer you want.
- C) Treat it as a successful, cheap finding — pivot toward the real pain or drop the idea.
- D) Raise money to push through.

---

## Answer key

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

1. **B** — learn, don't sell. (L1 §1)
2. **C** — past behavior beats hypotheticals. (L1 §2)
3. **B** — politeness destroys data. (L1 §3)
4. **B** — ~20%; silence draws out the real story. (L1 §5)
5. **C** — a tripped kill criterion is a cheap win; pivot or drop honestly. (L2 §4–5)

</details>
