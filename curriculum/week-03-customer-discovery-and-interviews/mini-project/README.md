# Mini-Project — The Discovery Dossier

> Assemble your interviews and synthesis into a single dossier. This is the evidence base for your capstone, and the part investors and judges trust most. Start it now with five interviews; grow it to ten or more by Week 10.

**Estimated time:** ~1.75 hours this week (plus the interviews themselves).

## What you will produce

A folder `discovery-dossier/` in your repo containing:

1. **`interviews/interview-0N.md`** — one write-up per interview (five this week, ten by capstone). No private identifiers.
2. **`dossier/SYNTHESIS.md`** — the running pattern across all interviews:
   - Segment(s) you talked to and how many.
   - The problem in customers' words.
   - Frequency and cost evidence (with counts/numbers where you have them).
   - The dominant current workaround.
   - Surprises, and whether any suggests a pivot.
   - Kill-criteria check (from Week 2) and a persevere/pivot/drop decision.

---

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] `discovery-dossier/interviews/` has at least five write-ups this week.
- [ ] Each write-up follows the Lecture 2 §3 structure and contains no private identifiers.
- [ ] `dossier/SYNTHESIS.md` exists with counts, the workaround, surprises, and a kill-criteria check.
- [ ] A clear persevere/pivot/drop decision with reasoning.
- [ ] Committed.

## Rubric

| Criterion | Weight |
|-----------|------:|
| Interviews use past behavior, not pitches (quotes show it) | 30% |
| Synthesis is honest — patterns are counted, not cherry-picked | 25% |
| Kill criteria are actually checked | 20% |
| The workaround and surprises are identified | 15% |
| Decision (persevere/pivot/drop) is justified | 10% |

---

## How this grows

You will add interviews in Weeks 4–9 toward ten or more. The dossier is graded again at the capstone, where ≥10 synthesized interviews are worth 25% of the rubric.
