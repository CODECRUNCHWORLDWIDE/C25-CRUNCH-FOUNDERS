# Week 3 Homework

Three problems plus the field work. The interviews dominate the week; these tie them together. Work in your Week 3 repo.

---

## Problem 1 — Recruit a pipeline

**Problem statement.** At `notes/recruiting-pipeline.md`, list at least ten realistic interview targets across at least two channels (warm network, community, referral, cold-but-relevant). For each: role/segment and how you will reach them. No private contact info in the file.

**Acceptance criteria.** File present; 10+ targets; 2+ channels; reach method per target. Committed.

**Hint.** If you cannot get to ten, that is a finding about your standing — note it.

**Estimated time.** 30 min.

---

## Problem 2 — Five interview write-ups

**Problem statement.** Run five interviews and write each up at `interviews/interview-0N.md` using the Lecture 2 §3 structure.

**Acceptance criteria.** Five files; each has problem-in-their-words, last-time story, cost, workaround, and a support/threat line. No private identifiers. Committed.

**Hint.** Write up within an hour of each call, before memory fades.

**Estimated time.** ~3.5 hours (counts as the week's field work).

---

## Problem 3 — First synthesis

**Problem statement.** At `notes/synthesis-v1.md`, cluster your five interviews into a pattern: is the problem real, frequent, costly? What is the shared workaround? What surprised you? Check against your Week 2 kill criteria and state: persevere, pivot, or drop.

**Acceptance criteria.** File present; pattern with counts; explicit kill-criteria check; a persevere/pivot/drop decision with reasoning. Committed.

**Hint.** A surprise that recurs across interviews is often the real idea. Follow it.

**Estimated time.** 45 min.
