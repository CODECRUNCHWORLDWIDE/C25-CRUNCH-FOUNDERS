# Week 3 — Homework

This week the **field work is the homework**. The five problems below tie the interviews together and feed straight into your [Discovery Dossier](./mini-project/README.md). Work in your Week 3 repo so each problem leaves a commit you can point to. Budget about **6 hours** total — the interviews dominate.

Each problem has a **problem statement**, **acceptance criteria**, a **hint**, and an **estimated time**. The grading rubric is at the bottom.

---

## Problem 1 — Recruit a real pipeline

**Problem statement.** At `discovery-dossier/recruiting-tracker.md`, build a recruiting funnel of **at least 15 realistic interview targets** across **at least three channels** (warm network, community, referral, cold-but-relevant). For each: role/segment, the channel, how you'll reach them, and a status column. Send the outreach this week and update statuses as replies come in. No private contact info in the file — segments and roles only.

**Acceptance criteria.**
- File present with 15+ targets and 3+ channels.
- A status column showing real funnel attrition (replied / scheduled / no-reply / no-show).
- Outreach actually sent (your statuses prove it).
- Committed.

**Hint.** You need to contact ~15–20 to land 5. If you genuinely cannot reach 15, that's a finding about your standing in this market — write a sentence on it and what you'd do about it.

**Estimated time.** 45 min (plus the outreach trickling in over the week).

## Problem 2 — De-biased interview script

**Problem statement.** Complete [Exercise 1](./exercises/exercise-01-build-and-debias-your-script.md): your idea reframed as a problem, a non-pitch frame, 6–8 past-behavior questions, and a bias audit catching and rewriting at least one bad question. Save to `discovery-dossier/interview-script.md`.

**Acceptance criteria.**
- Idea stated as a **problem**, not a solution.
- 6–8 questions, all anchored in past behavior / cost / workarounds.
- A bias audit covering **every** question, with ≥1 caught and rewritten.
- Zero questions that pitch your solution.
- Committed.

**Hint.** Hand the script to a cohort-mate and ask them to find the pitch. If they find one, your audit missed it.

**Estimated time.** 50 min.

---

## Problem 3 — Five interview write-ups

**Problem statement.** Run **five** discovery interviews and write each up at `discovery-dossier/interviews/interview-0N.md` using the [Exercise 2 template](./exercises/exercise-02-interview-writeup-template.md). Same-day write-ups. No private identifiers.

**Acceptance criteria.**
- Five write-ups present.
- Each has: problem-in-their-words (verbatim quote), a specific last-time story, cost, current workaround, signal noted honestly, and a support/threaten line.
- At least three contain a genuine past-behavior story (the quotes prove it).
- A referral was asked in each (note who they pointed to, no identifiers).
- Committed.

**Hint.** Write up within an hour of each call, before memory fades and flatters. The interview that threatened your idea gets the same care as the one that flattered it.

**Estimated time.** ~3.5 hours (this is the week's field work).

---

## Problem 4 — Synthesis and the decision

**Problem statement.** Complete the [Exercise 3 canvas](./exercises/exercise-03-synthesis-and-pattern-canvas.md) at `discovery-dossier/dossier/SYNTHESIS.md`: cluster findings into counted themes, plot frequency vs. severity, name the dominant workaround and the surprise, check **every** Week 2 kill criterion against the evidence, and end with an explicit **persevere / pivot / drop** decision and reasoning.

**Acceptance criteria.**
- Themes with **independent-mention counts**.
- Frequency × severity placement for each theme.
- Dominant workaround named precisely.
- Surprise named honestly (or "no surprise, here's why").
- Each kill criterion checked as written — no moved goalposts.
- An explicit decision with reasoning.
- Committed.

**Hint.** If every theme has exactly one mention, you don't have a pattern yet — that usually means "keep interviewing toward ten," which is itself a legitimate answer here.

**Estimated time.** 1 hour.

---

## Problem 5 — Reflection

**Problem statement.** Write a 300–400 word reflection at `notes/week-03-reflection.md` answering, each in its own paragraph:

1. Where did you catch yourself pitching or asking a leading question — and how did you recover mid-interview?
2. What did the interviews *change* about your idea? Name a specific Week 2 assumption that the data confirmed and one it threatened.
3. Did a surprise emerge? If so, what — and are you treating it as noise or a pivot, and why?
4. What's the single biggest open question your five interviews didn't resolve, and what's your plan to resolve it on the way to ten?

**Acceptance criteria.**
- 300–400 words, each numbered question in its own paragraph.
- Honest and specific (this is for future-you, not for show).
- Committed.

**Hint.** The most valuable reflections name the moment the data made you uncomfortable. That discomfort is usually where the truth was.

**Estimated time.** 30 min.

## Grading rubric

Each problem is scored 0–4, then weighted. Total out of 100.

| Problem | Weight | 4 (excellent) | 2 (passing) | 0 (missing) |
|---------|------:|---------------|-------------|-------------|
| **1 — Pipeline** | 15% | 15+ targets, 3+ channels, real attrition shown | 10+ targets, some channels | No funnel / fabricated |
| **2 — Script** | 20% | Problem-framed, fully bias-audited, zero pitches, ≥1 rewrite | Mostly past-behavior, minor leading questions remain | Pitches the solution / no audit |
| **3 — Write-ups** | 30% | 5 vivid write-ups, verbatim quotes, specific costs, no identifiers, includes a threatening one | 5 present but thin or paraphrased | Fewer than 5 / private identifiers / pure pitches |
| **4 — Synthesis** | 25% | Counted themes, freq≠severity, workaround + surprise named, every criterion checked, clear decision | Pattern stated but uncounted; partial kill-criteria check | No synthesis / goalposts moved |
| **5 — Reflection** | 10% | Honest, specific, names the uncomfortable moment | Present but generic | Missing |

**Scoring notes.**
- A **drop** or **pivot** decision earns full marks in Problem 4 when the reasoning is rigorous. You are graded on honest method, not on your idea surviving.
- Any write-up containing a private identifier (name, email, employer-specific detail) caps Problem 3 at 2 regardless of quality — privacy is non-negotiable.
- Showing a mockup or pitching in an interview caps Problem 3 at 2 — that's a sales call, not discovery.

**Passing bar:** 70/100, with Problems 3 and 4 each at 2 or above (the interviews and the synthesis are the heart of the week — you cannot pass by skipping them).
