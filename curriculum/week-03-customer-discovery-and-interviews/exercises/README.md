# Week 3 — Exercises

These are not coding exercises — this is a founder course. The exercises this week are **worksheets, templates, and canvases** you fill in with your own idea and your own interview data. They are deliberately sequenced: Exercise 1 produces the script you'll use in your interviews, Exercise 2 is the template you fill in *after each interview*, and Exercise 3 is the canvas you complete *once* at the end of the week to synthesize everything. Together they feed directly into the [Discovery Dossier mini-project](../mini-project/README.md).

Each one is a real artifact, not busywork. You'll reuse Exercise 1's script and Exercise 2's template every week from here to the capstone.

## Index

| # | Exercise | Type | Est. time | Output |
|---|----------|------|-----------|--------|
| 1 | **[Build & de-bias your interview script](exercise-01-build-and-debias-your-script.md)** | Guided worksheet | ~50 min | A ready-to-use script with a bias audit |
| 2 | **[Interview write-up template](exercise-02-interview-writeup-template.md)** | Reusable template | ~10 min/interview | One filled write-up per interview |
| 3 | **[Synthesis & pattern canvas](exercise-03-synthesis-and-pattern-canvas.md)** | Canvas | ~90 min | A completed synthesis with a decision |

Do them in order. Exercise 1 before any interview; Exercise 2 right after each interview; Exercise 3 once you have at least five write-ups.

## How to work the exercises

- **No pitching, ever.** If your script mentions your solution, it's broken. Discovery learns; it does not sell.
- **Past behavior over hypotheticals.** Every question should anchor to something that actually happened. If a question can be answered "yeah, probably," rewrite it.
- **Use the customer's exact words.** In write-ups, quote verbatim. Your paraphrase loses the data and the emotion.
- **Let the idea fail.** Keep your Week 2 kill criteria in view. An exercise that can only confirm your idea is theater.
- **Commit everything to your Week 3 repo.** Script, write-ups, and synthesis live in `discovery-dossier/` as Markdown. Evidence in version control beats memory.

## Checklist for the week

Work this top to bottom. Field work has lead time — the recruiting items must start Monday.

- [ ] **Exercise 1 done:** script written, every question bias-audited, zero pitches.
- [ ] **Recruiting started (Monday):** outreach sent to ≥15 prospects; tracker spreadsheet created.
- [ ] **Calendar filling:** at least 5 interviews booked by Tuesday night.
- [ ] **Interview 1 run + written up same day** using the Exercise 2 template.
- [ ] **Interviews 2–5 run + written up**, each same day, no private identifiers.
- [ ] **Referral asked in every interview** ("who else should I talk to?").
- [ ] **Exercise 3 synthesis completed:** findings clustered, mentions counted, frequency vs. severity plotted, workaround named, surprise identified.
- [ ] **Kill criteria checked** against the evidence — explicitly, each one.
- [ ] **Persevere / pivot / drop decision written down** with reasoning.
- [ ] **Everything committed** to `discovery-dossier/` in your Week 3 repo.

There are no checked-in solutions — this is *your* idea and *your* data. After the week, compare notes with cohort-mates: did your bias audits catch the same traps? Did anyone's interviews force a pivot?
