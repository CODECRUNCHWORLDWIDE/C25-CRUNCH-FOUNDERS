# Exercise 2 — Interview Write-up Template

**Type:** Reusable template (fill in once per interview).
**Estimated time:** ~10 minutes per interview — done the **same day**, ideally within the hour.
**Output:** One file per interview at `discovery-dossier/interviews/interview-0N.md`.

---

## How to use this

This is a **template**, not a one-time worksheet. Copy the block below into a new file after *every* interview and fill it in while the conversation is fresh. Memory decays within hours and — worse — it flatters: you'll remember the parts that confirmed your idea and forget the parts that threatened it. The write-up is how the evidence survives.

Rules:

- **Same day, every time.** A write-up you "do later" is a write-up you do badly.
- **No private identifiers.** Use a role and segment ("ER nurse, mid-size urban hospital"), never a name, email, or anything that identifies a specific person.
- **Quote verbatim.** Their exact words in quotation marks. "It's a nightmare every Monday" is data; "the user experiences Monday friction" is your gloss losing the data.
- **Write up the bad ones too.** The interview that threatened your idea is the one your future self most needs and most wants to forget.

---

## The template (copy everything below into `interview-0N.md`)

```markdown
# Interview 0N — <segment / role, no name>

- **Date:** YYYY-MM-DD
- **Segment / role:** (e.g., "office manager, independent dental clinic, ~6 staff")
- **How I found them:** (warm network / referral from interview 0X / community / cold)
- **Recorded?** (yes, with consent / no — notes only)
- **Interview length:** ~NN min

## Context — who they are and why they have the problem
<1–2 sentences. Enough that a stranger reading this later understands why this person is relevant.>

## The problem, in their words
> "<verbatim quote describing the problem>"
> "<another verbatim quote if you have one>"

## The last-time story
<What actually happened the last time the problem occurred. The specific event, not a generic average. What did they do? What did it cost — time, money, stress?>

## Current workaround / spend
- **What they use today:** <tool, process, person, spreadsheet, nothing>
- **What it costs:** <$/month, hours/week, headcount, or "nothing">
- **What's missing / what breaks:** <the gap in the current solution>

## Signal — did they commit anything real?
<Time (follow-up offered?), reputation (a referral?), money (willingness/spend?), or none.
"Said it sounds nice" is NOT signal — note that honestly if that's all you got.>

## Surprises
<Anything you didn't expect. Especially flag it if it's something you've now heard more than once.>

## Frequency & severity (your read)
- **Frequency:** <how often this happens for them — daily / weekly / monthly / yearly>
- **Severity:** <how much it hurts when it does — mild annoyance / costs real money / loses a customer>

## Support or threaten?
<One line: does this interview SUPPORT or THREATEN my idea? Does it trip any Week 2 kill criterion? Which one?>

## Referral
<Who did they point me to? (no identifiers — "their billing colleague," "a friend at another clinic")>
```

---

## Worked example (so you know the bar)

A filled write-up should read like a small documentary of a real event. Sketch of a good one:

> **Problem, in their words:** *"When someone cancels the morning of, that slot is just dead. I'll text a couple people I know are waiting but half the time nobody picks up and the chair sits empty."*
>
> **Last-time story:** Last Tuesday a 2 p.m. crown cancelled at 9 a.m. She personally texted four people from a paper waitlist in a drawer, got one reply, who couldn't make it. Slot sat empty — she quoted the lost revenue figure for a specialist chair-hour.
>
> **Workaround:** A paper list in a drawer + personal texting. Costs ~20 min per cancellation and usually fails. The two reminder tools they pay for don't touch waitlists.
>
> **Signal:** Offered to introduce two other clinic managers (reputation). Asked, unprompted, "does the thing you're building do *that*?" — caught herself leaning in.
>
> **Surprise:** I came in about *no-shows*; she kept returning to *late cancellations and the empty slot*. Third interviewee to do this.
>
> **Support/threaten:** Threatens my original "reminder" framing; strongly supports a "waitlist auto-fill" pivot. Does NOT trip kill criteria — frequent and costly.

## Acceptance criteria (per interview)

- [ ] File at `discovery-dossier/interviews/interview-0N.md`, committed the same day.
- [ ] No private identifiers anywhere.
- [ ] At least one **verbatim quote** in the problem section.
- [ ] A specific **last-time story**, not a generic average.
- [ ] Workaround and spend captured (even if the answer is "nothing").
- [ ] Signal noted honestly (including "just politeness" if that's the truth).
- [ ] Frequency and severity both estimated.
- [ ] A one-line support/threaten + kill-criteria check.

---

## Hints

- If your "problem in their words" section has no quotation marks, you paraphrased — go back to your notes and pull the real words.
- If every interview "supports" your idea, you're probably not threatening it hard enough. Re-read your questions for hidden pitches.
- Number files with a leading zero (`interview-01.md`) so they sort correctly past nine.
