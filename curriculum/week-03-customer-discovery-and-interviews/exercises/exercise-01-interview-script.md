# Exercise 1 — Interview Script

**Goal:** Write a discovery interview script for your chosen idea, then audit it for leading questions and pitches.

**Estimated time:** 45 minutes.

---

## What to produce

Create `exercises/exercise-01-interview-script.md` with:

1. **A one-sentence frame** you will open with (research, not a pitch).
2. **6–8 questions**, all anchored in past behavior, cost, and workarounds.
3. **A bias audit table** — for each question, mark whether it is leading or hypothetical, and rewrite it if so.

```markdown
# Discovery script — <idea, as a problem>

## Frame
"I'm researching how people handle ___. No pitch; I just want to learn."

## Questions
1. Tell me about the last time ___ happened.
2. What did you do about it? What did that cost you (time/money)?
3. What do you use today to handle this?
...

## Bias audit
| # | Leading/hypothetical? | Rewrite |
|---|------------------------|---------|
| 1 | No | — |
| 2 | No | — |
```

---

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] File at `exercises/exercise-01-interview-script.md`.
- [ ] A non-pitch frame.
- [ ] 6–8 past-behavior questions.
- [ ] A bias audit with at least one question rewritten.
- [ ] No question that pitches your solution.
- [ ] Committed.

---

## Hints

- If a question can be answered "yes/sounds good," it is probably a hypothetical. Rewrite it to ask what actually happened.
- Keep the solution out entirely. You can show it in a *later* (validation) interview, not a discovery one.
