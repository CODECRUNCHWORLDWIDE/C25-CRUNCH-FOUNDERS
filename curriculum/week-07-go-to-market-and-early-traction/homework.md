# Week 7 Homework

Five problems that revisit the week's topics and feed your mini-project. The full set should take about **6 hours**, most of it the real channel test (Problem 3). Work in your Week 7 Git repo so each problem produces at least one commit you can point to. And keep interviewing toward your capstone target of ten discovery interviews — GTM and discovery aren't separate; every outreach this week is also a conversation.

Each problem includes a **problem statement**, **acceptance criteria**, a **hint**, and an **estimated time**.

---

## Problem 1 — Motion-fit memo

**Problem statement.** At `notes/motion-fit.md`, argue why your chosen go-to-market motion fits your **price/ACV**, your **customer reachability**, and your **sales complexity**. Then name one motion you are *deliberately not* using and explain why it's a poor fit. Reference your Week 6 price explicitly. Use the price-point ladder from Lecture 1.

**Acceptance criteria.**

- File present.
- Fit argued across all three dimensions (price/ACV, reachability, complexity).
- One rejected motion named, with a concrete reason it doesn't fit.
- Your actual Week-6 price/ACV appears in the argument.
- Committed.

**Hint.** A cheap product can't afford an expensive motion, and a tiny nameable market doesn't need ads. If your motion and your model are different "shapes" (high-touch motion + low-price model, or vice versa), that's the channel/model misfit — flag it.

**Estimated time.** 30 minutes.

## Problem 2 — Vanity-vs-actionable rewrite

**Problem statement.** At `notes/metrics-rewrite.md`, list **five** metrics someone might be tempted to track for your startup. For each, classify it as vanity or actionable using the three-question test, and for every vanity one, write the **actionable rewrite** (turn the total into a rate). Then name your **One Metric That Matters** for your current stage and justify it in two sentences.

**Acceptance criteria.**

- File present with five metrics classified.
- Each classification justified against at least one of the three test questions.
- Every vanity metric has an actionable rewrite (a rate or behavior).
- Your OMTM named and justified for your stage.
- Committed.

**Hint.** The fix for almost any vanity metric is to turn a cumulative total into a rate: "10,000 views" → "5% of views start a trial." Tie your OMTM to money or repeated core-action behavior.

**Estimated time.** 45 minutes.

## Problem 3 — Run the channel test (the week's field work)

**Problem statement.** Run **one** small, time-boxed channel test — the one you designed in Exercise 2. Contact **≥ 30 real prospects** (or run an equivalent real smoke test / content push), with a **pre-registered success bar** set *before* you start. Log everything at `notes/channel-test.md` and a tracking sheet: the channel, the specific action, the time box, the pre-set bar, and the full funnel numbers with dates and conversion rates.

**Acceptance criteria.**

- File + tracking sheet present.
- A real channel test against ≥ 30 real prospects (or a clearly-labeled legitimate proxy).
- Pre-registered, numeric, falsifiable success bar — set before running (visible in commit history or dated).
- Funnel logged with every step, dates, and conversion rates between steps.
- A plain verdict: did it clear the bar?
- Committed.

**Hint.** Do things that don't scale — manual, personalized outreach is expected and correct. In 2026, 30 well-researched personalized emails beat 300 generic ones. Log each step *as it happens*; don't reconstruct from memory at the end.

**Estimated time.** ~2.5 hours of active work (spread over 1–2 days while replies come in).

## Problem 4 — Funnel read + leak diagnosis

**Problem statement.** At `notes/funnel-read.md`, analyze your Problem 3 funnel. Compute the conversion rate between every step. Identify the leakiest step **by rate** (not raw count). State whether the leak is a **channel problem** (top of funnel) or a **product/onboarding problem** (activation), what that implies, and the **next experiment** you'd run with a new pre-registered bar. Then compute your **blended CAC** (cash + valued time) and check **LTV:CAC** and rough payback against your Week 6 LTV.

**Acceptance criteria.**

- File present.
- Conversion rate computed for every step.
- Leakiest step named by rate, with channel-vs-product diagnosis.
- A next experiment proposed with a new bar.
- Blended CAC computed; LTV:CAC and payback checked against Week 6, with the small-sample caveat noted.
- Committed.

**Hint.** A leak at *activation* often means problem/solution fit is still off — people show up but don't get value — and no channel change fixes that. Value your own time in the CAC; "$0 cash" is not "free."

**Estimated time.** 45 minutes.

## Problem 5 — Reflection

**Problem statement.** Write a 300–400 word reflection at `notes/week-07-reflection.md` answering:

1. Did your channel test clear its bar? How did running it change what you believe about reaching your customers?
2. What was the leakiest step, and were you surprised by where it was?
3. Did you feel the pull of any failure mode this week — chasing a vanity metric, wanting to extend a failing test, channel-hopping, or under-personalizing outreach? How did you handle it?
4. What's your single most important next GTM move, and by when?

**Acceptance criteria.**

- File exists, 300–400 words.
- Each numbered question addressed in its own paragraph.
- Committed.

**Hint.** This is for you, not for a grade. Be honest about what flopped — a test that missed its bar but taught you something is a *success* of process. Future-you reading this before demo day will be grateful.

**Estimated time.** 30 minutes.

## Grading rubric

Homework is graded out of **100 points**. The weighting reflects that the *real test* (Problem 3) and *reading it honestly* (Problem 4) are the heart of the week.

| Criterion | Points | What earns full marks |
|-----------|------:|------------------------|
| **Motion fit (P1)** | 15 | Motion justified across price/ACV, reachability, and complexity; a rejected motion named with a real reason; Week-6 price referenced. |
| **Metric discipline (P2)** | 15 | Five metrics correctly classified via the three-question test; every vanity one rewritten as a rate; a defensible OMTM for the stage. |
| **A real test was run (P3)** | 30 | ≥30 real contacts (or labeled legitimate proxy); pre-registered, falsifiable bar set *before*; full funnel with dates and rates; honest verdict. |
| **Funnel read honestly (P4)** | 25 | Rates computed for every step; leakiest step found by rate; correct channel-vs-product diagnosis; CAC/LTV:CAC/payback checked vs. Week 6 with the small-sample caveat. |
| **Reflection (P5)** | 10 | All four questions answered honestly in their own paragraphs; names a real failure-mode pull and a dated next move. |
| **Craft & honesty** | 5 | Committed cleanly; numbers internally consistent; no goalpost-moving; estimates labeled as estimates. |

**Score bands:** 90–100 = ready to present this as a traction story at demo day. 75–89 = solid; tighten the funnel read or the test rigor. 60–74 = the test was thin or the funnel wasn't read by rate — redo Problem 3 or 4. Below 60 = no real test was run; this is the week's whole point — go run it.

A note on grading philosophy: **a test that honestly missed its bar scores higher than a vague "promising" result with no bar.** We grade the rigor of the process, not the flattery of the outcome. A missed bar reported honestly *is* a passing answer.
