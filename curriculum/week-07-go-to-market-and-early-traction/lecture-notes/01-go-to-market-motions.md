# Lecture 1 — Go-to-Market Motions

> **Duration:** ~1 hour. **Outcome:** You can name the main go-to-market motions and match one to your model.

## 1. What "motion" means

A go-to-market motion is the repeatable way customers come to you. The main ones:

- **Founder-led / direct sales** — you sell person-to-person. Right for higher-priced, fewer, reachable customers (especially B2B early on).
- **Community-led** — you grow inside a community that has the problem; trust spreads by word of mouth.
- **Content / inbound** — you publish useful material that pulls in people searching for the problem. Slow to start, compounding over time.
- **Paid acquisition** — you buy attention via ads. Needs known, positive unit economics or you scale a loss.
- **Partnerships / channel** — another company's reach distributes you.

## 2. Match the motion to the model

The motion must fit price, customer, and sales complexity:

- Low price, huge audience → self-serve + content or paid (you cannot afford to hand-sell a $5 product).
- High price, few customers → founder-led sales (a personal touch is affordable and necessary).
- Niche community with the problem → community-led often beats everything.

A mismatch (expensive sales motion for a cheap product, or ads for a tiny niche) burns money and time.

## 3. Do things that don't scale, first

Early traction usually comes from unscalable effort: emailing people one by one, onboarding the first customers by hand, hanging out where they already are. This is not a failure to "automate" — it is how you learn the channel and earn the first believers. Automate only once a manual motion clearly works.

## 4. Pick one to test now

You do not run five motions at once early on; you pick the single best-fit motion and test it deliberately this week. Spreading across channels with no money or focus produces noise, not learning.
