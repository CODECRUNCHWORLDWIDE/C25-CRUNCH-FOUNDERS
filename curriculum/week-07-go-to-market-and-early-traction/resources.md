# Week 7 — Resources

Every resource on this page is **free** to read or watch. Essays from founders and investors, free chapters and talks, and open reference material. No paywalled courses are required. Where a great resource is a paid book, we link the free essay or talk that covers the same idea.

A note on bias: every framework below was written by someone with a worldview. Y Combinator's advice assumes you might raise venture money; a bootstrapper's advice assumes you won't. Read across the spread and take what fits *your* model — the one you built in Week 6.

## Start here (work these into Monday and Tuesday)

- **Paul Graham — "Do Things That Don't Scale."** The single most important essay for early-stage GTM. Why the founder must recruit the first users by hand, and why "it doesn't scale" is the wrong objection at the start.
  <https://paulgraham.com/ds.html>
- **Brian Balfour — Traction & Growth essay series (brianbalfour.com).** The clearest writing on channel/model fit — why a channel that works for one company fails for another.
  <https://brianbalfour.com/essays>
- **Lenny Rachitsky — "How the fastest-growing startups find their first customers."** A survey of how real companies got their first users; pattern-matches well to the five motions.
  <https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/how-the-fastest-growing-companies>
- **Y Combinator — Startup School (free).** YC's talks on early growth and talking to users are directly on-topic and free with no signup required.
  <https://www.startupschool.org/>

## Go-to-market motions and channels

- **Gabriel Weinberg & Justin Mares — *Traction* (the "Bullseye" framework).** The book is paid, but the core idea — list all 19 channels, rank them, test three, focus on one — is summarized free in the authors' talks and interviews. The canonical "how do I pick a channel" tool.
  Author talk search: <https://duckduckgo.com/?q=traction+gabriel+weinberg+bullseye+framework+talk>
- **Brian Balfour — "Why Product Market Fit Isn't Enough" (Four Fits).** Product/market, market/channel, channel/model, model/market — why all four must align. The reason a great product still can't find customers.
  <https://brianbalfour.com/essays/product-market-fit-isnt-enough>
- **First Round Review — founder-led sales archive.** Practical, reported pieces on how technical founders learn to sell the first deals themselves.
  <https://review.firstround.com/>
- **a16z — go-to-market essay library.** Andreessen Horowitz's free essays on sales-led vs. product-led motions for B2B. Read with a grain of salt — they fund big-swing companies.
  <https://a16z.com/tag/go-to-market/>

## Metrics, funnels, and traction

- **Dave McClure — "Startup Metrics for Pirates (AARRR)."** The original deck that named Acquisition, Activation, Retention, Referral, Revenue. The skeleton of every funnel you'll build this week.
  <https://www.slideshare.net/dmc500hats/startup-metrics-for-pirates-long-version>
- **Alistair Croll & Benjamin Yoskovitz — *Lean Analytics* ("One Metric That Matters").** The book is paid; the OMTM concept and the "stages of a startup" model are covered free in the authors' talks and the companion site.
  <https://leananalyticsbook.com/>
- **Eric Ries — "Vanity Metrics vs. Actionable Metrics."** From *The Lean Startup*; the three tests for whether a metric is real, covered free in his talks and the Lean Startup principles page.
  <https://theleanstartup.com/principles>
- **Andrew Chen — "The Law of Shitty Clickthroughs."** Why every channel decays over time, and what that means for a founder betting on one channel. Free on his blog.
  <https://andrewchen.com/the-law-of-shitty-clickthroughs/>
- **Amplitude — North Star Metric playbook (free).** A modern, practical guide to picking the one metric a team aligns on. Vendor-published but framework-neutral and well-edited.
  <https://amplitude.com/blog/product-north-star-metric>

## Unit economics carried over from Week 6 (keep these open)

- **David Skok — "SaaS Metrics 2.0" (forEntrepreneurs).** The reference for CAC, LTV, LTV:CAC, and CAC payback. You'll hold this week's channel-test spend against these numbers.
  <https://www.forentrepreneurs.com/saas-metrics-2/>
- **Reference rules of thumb (2026):** an LTV:CAC of roughly 3:1 is the common health target; CAC payback under ~12 months is comfortable for early B2B SaaS, under ~18 acceptable. Treat these as conversation-starters, not laws — your model decides.

## Modern (2024–2026) reality checks

- **The decline of organic reach and cold-email reply rates.** AI-assisted outbound flooded inboxes; reply rates on generic cold email fell through 2024–2025. The takeaway for your test: a narrow, named, personalized list beats volume. Search current data; treat any single number skeptically.
  <https://duckduckgo.com/?q=cold+email+reply+rates+2025+decline>
- **Founder-led content as a 2026 default channel.** On LinkedIn, X, and short-form video, the founder's own voice now often out-converts the company account for early B2B. Lenny's newsletter and First Round both cover this; see their archives above.
- **Community-led growth (Discord/Slack) maturing.** The "community as a moat" thesis cooled after 2022's hype; what survived is communities that solve a real recurring need. Apply the same skepticism you'd apply to any motion.

## Glossary cheat sheet

Keep this open in a tab.

| Term | Plain English |
|------|---------------|
| **GTM motion** | The repeatable *system* by which customers come to you (e.g. founder-led sales). Not a single tactic. |
| **Channel** | One specific path inside a motion (e.g. cold email; SEO; a partner's referral). |
| **Campaign** | One time-boxed push inside a channel (e.g. "this two-week outreach to 40 clinics"). |
| **Founder-led sales** | Selling person-to-person, unscalably, early on — the founder *is* the sales team. |
| **CAC** | Customer Acquisition Cost — total spend (money + valued time) to win one paying customer. |
| **LTV** | Lifetime Value — total contribution margin you expect from a customer over their life. |
| **LTV:CAC** | The ratio. ~3:1 is a common health target; below 1:1 means you lose money per customer. |
| **Payback period** | Months of margin to earn back the CAC. Under ~12 months is comfortable early. |
| **OMTM / North Star** | The One Metric That Matters at your current stage; the number you optimize now. |
| **Vanity metric** | A number that goes up regardless of real progress (impressions, cumulative signups). |
| **Actionable metric** | A metric that changes behavior, is comparable over time, usually a rate or ratio. |
| **AARRR** | Pirate metrics: Acquisition, Activation, Retention, Referral, Revenue — a funnel skeleton. |
| **Activation** | The moment a new user first experiences the product's core value ("aha"). |
| **Retention** | Whether users come back; the truest early signal of real value. |
| **Leading indicator** | A metric that predicts future results (demos booked). Move-able now. |
| **Lagging indicator** | A metric that reports past results (revenue). Confirms, doesn't steer. |
| **Conversion rate** | Of the people who reached step N, the fraction that reached step N+1. |
| **Leakiest step** | The biggest drop in the funnel — fix it first. |
| **Smoke test / fake door** | A test of demand for something not yet built (a landing page with a "buy" button). |
