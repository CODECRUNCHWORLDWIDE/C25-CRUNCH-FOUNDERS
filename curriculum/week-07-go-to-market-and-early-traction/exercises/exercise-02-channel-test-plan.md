# Exercise 2 — Channel Test Plan (Worksheet / Template)

**Goal:** Design the cheap, time-boxed channel experiment you'll actually run on Wednesday–Thursday — with a success bar you register *before* you see any data, and a decision rule for every possible outcome.

**Estimated time:** 40 minutes to plan. (The test itself runs over the following days.)

**Format:** A fill-in worksheet/template. Copy the template at the bottom into `exercises/exercise-02-channel-test-plan.md` in your Week 7 repo and complete every field. This is not code — it's the operating plan for your traction test.

---

## How to use this worksheet

Work top to bottom. The order matters: you choose the channel, define exactly what you'll do, set the time box, **then** register the success bar before reasoning about outcomes. The whole point is to commit to "what counts as a win" while you're still honest — before the data tempts you to move the goalposts.

A filled example (Maria's ClinicFlow) follows each section in a quote block so you can see the altitude of a good answer.

---

## Section A — The channel (one only)

Pick **one** channel from your inner ring (Lecture 1's Bullseye). Not three. State the channel and the one-line reason it's worth testing first (usually: most learning per hour, or most reachable volume).

> **Maria:** Channel = personal cold email to named clinics. Reason to test first: highest reachable volume of qualified, nameable prospects, and the channel I'll lean on most if it works.

## Section B — The specific action

Define exactly what you will do, concretely enough that an outsider could verify you did it. Include the target list size and how you'll build it.

> **Maria:** Email 40 named independent clinics in the Austin metro. List built from the state dental + medical board directories, filtered to solo/2-doctor practices (my ICP). Each email personalized with the clinic's name and one specific detail (e.g., "saw your practice takes new patients on Mondays"). No templated blasts.

## Section C — The message / offer

What will you actually say? Draft the core of the outreach — the hook, the value, and the single call to action. One clear CTA, not three.

> **Maria's email skeleton:**
> - **Hook:** "Quick question about no-shows at [Clinic] —"
> - **Value:** "We help small clinics cut no-shows ~30% with automated reminders that take 15 minutes to set up."
> - **CTA (one):** "Worth a 15-minute demo this week? Here's my calendar: [link]."
> - **Proof:** one line — "Two Austin clinics already using it."

## Section D — The time box

State the start date, end date, and total duration. One to two weeks is right — long enough for a signal, short enough to force a decision.

> **Maria:** Start Wed, end the following Fri. **10 working days.** No extensions (see decision rule).

## Section E — The funnel I'll record

List the exact steps you'll log, in order, and where you'll log them (a sheet with one row per prospect). This is what turns activity into evidence.

> **Maria's funnel + tracking columns (one row per clinic):**
> `Clinic | Contacted (date) | Replied? | Demo booked? | Demo done? | Trial started? | Paid? | Notes`
> Funnel steps: Contacted → Replied → Demo → Trial → Paid.

## Section F — The success bar (PRE-REGISTERED — fill before you run)

This is the heart of the exercise. **Before any data exists,** write what counts as a win. Make it a specific number tied to your OMTM. Sign and date it.

> **Maria (registered Tue, before sending):** "Win = **≥ 2 paid onboardings OR ≥ 4 trials started** from 40 contacts in 10 days. Anything less is not a win, no matter how 'promising' the replies feel. — M.R., [date]."

A good bar is:
- **Numeric** (a count or rate, not "some interest").
- **Tied to the OMTM** (paid onboardings, not likes).
- **Set in advance** and dated.
- **Falsifiable** — there's a clearly-defined way to *not* hit it.

## Section G — The decision rule (every outcome)

Decide *now* what you'll do for each outcome, so Friday-you can't rationalize. Cover three cases:

1. **Hit the bar** → what scaling move comes next?
2. **Missed, but the funnel shows a fixable leak** → fix *what*, and re-run once on a *fresh* list?
3. **Missed with no signal at all** (e.g., near-zero replies) → kill this channel and move to which inner-ring channel next?

> **Maria's decision rule:**
> 1. **Hit** (≥2 paid / ≥4 trials) → scale cold email to 200 clinics across three metros next sprint.
> 2. **Miss, leak at demo→trial** → rebuild the demo around their real no-show numbers + same-day trial setup; re-run on 40 *fresh* clinics, once.
> 3. **Miss, no replies (<3 of 40)** → cold email isn't the channel; switch to the practice-management forum (community motion) as the next inner-ring test.

## Section H — Cost (to compute CAC later)

Estimate the spend: cash + the value of your time. You'll divide this by customers won to get a blended CAC and check it against last week's LTV.

> **Maria:** Cash ≈ $0 (free tools). Time ≈ 20 hrs over 10 days. Valued at $50/hr → **$1,000 of "spend."** If I win 2 customers → blended CAC ≈ $500; against LTV ~$1,400 that's LTV:CAC ≈ 2.8:1. Acceptable, improves with practice.

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] Exactly **one** channel chosen, with a reason.
- [ ] A specific, verifiable action with a target list size and sourcing method.
- [ ] Draft message with a single clear CTA.
- [ ] A time box with start/end dates and "no extensions" stated.
- [ ] The exact funnel steps and tracking columns listed.
- [ ] A **pre-registered, numeric, falsifiable** success bar tied to your OMTM, dated.
- [ ] A decision rule covering hit / miss-with-leak / miss-no-signal.
- [ ] A cost estimate (cash + valued time) ready to compute CAC.
- [ ] Committed to your Week 7 repo.

## Common mistakes

- **Vague success bar** ("get some traction"). If it can't be falsified, it can't be a bar.
- **Testing three channels at once.** You won't be able to tell which worked. One.
- **A templated blast instead of a real action.** In 2026, 40 personalized emails beat 4,000 generic ones. Quality of list and message is the test.
- **Leaving extensions open.** "Let's give it one more week" is how dead channels eat your runway. Box it.
- **No tracking sheet.** Untracked outreach is not an experiment; it's just activity.
