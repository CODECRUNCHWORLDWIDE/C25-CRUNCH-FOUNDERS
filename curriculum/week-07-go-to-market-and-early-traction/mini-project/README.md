# Mini-Project — The Go-to-Market One-Pager

> Write a single page that names your go-to-market motion, the first channel you tested, the funnel result, and the one traction metric you will hold yourself to. It feeds the traction slide of your capstone pitch.

**Estimated time:** ~1.75 hours.

## What you will produce

A file `gtm/GTM-ONE-PAGER.md` with:

1. **Customer & motion** — who you sell to and the chosen motion, with the fit reasoning.
2. **The one metric that matters** — and the vanity metrics you are ignoring.
3. **Channel test result** — the action, time box, pre-set bar, funnel numbers, and whether it cleared the bar.
4. **Leakiest step & next experiment** — where the funnel leaks and what you will try next.
5. **The traction commitment** — the single metric and the target you will be accountable to over the next month.

Keep it to one page. A GTM plan that needs five pages this early is mostly guessing.

---

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] File at `gtm/GTM-ONE-PAGER.md`, one page.
- [ ] Motion named with fit reasoning.
- [ ] One metric chosen; vanity metrics set aside.
- [ ] Channel test result with funnel numbers and pre-set bar.
- [ ] Leakiest step and next experiment.
- [ ] A specific traction commitment.
- [ ] Committed.

## Rubric

| Criterion | Weight |
|-----------|------:|
| Motion fits price/customer/complexity | 25% |
| The chosen metric is actionable, not vanity | 25% |
| Channel test is real, with a pre-set bar | 20% |
| Funnel is read for its leakiest step | 20% |
| The traction commitment is specific | 10% |
